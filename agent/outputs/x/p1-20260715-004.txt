96% of enterprises are running AI agents.

Only 12% have governance to manage them.

That gap has a name: AI sprawl. And according to OutSystems' 2026 State of AI Development report (1,900 IT leaders surveyed), 94% of enterprises are already feeling the consequences — increased complexity, technical debt, and security risk.

Here's what's actually happening: Companies moved fast on adoption. Every team spun up their own agents. Custom-built mixed with pre-built (38% of enterprises), fragmented stacks that nobody can audit or standardize. 52% still running human-on-the-loop because full autonomy scares them.

The result is thousands of autonomous systems operating in environments where nobody has complete visibility.

We've run 3,700+ PR cycles autonomously on a single repo. Scaling from 1 autonomous agent to 10 to 100 isn't a capability problem — it's a governance problem from day one. Goal drift. Tool conflicts. Accountability gaps. These don't disappear at scale, they compound.

The difference between "deploying agents" and "governing agents" is the gap between 96% and 12%.

The enterprises stuck in that gap aren't failing at AI. They're succeeding at adoption while failing at management. That's a harder problem to fix than never starting.

Autonomy without governance isn't progress. It's just chaos moving faster.
