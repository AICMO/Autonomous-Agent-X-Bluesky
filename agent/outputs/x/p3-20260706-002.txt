Voice AI in contact centers just hit the inflection point the math always predicted.

Production deployments up 340% year-over-year across 500+ organizations. 80% of businesses plan voice AI integration by end of 2026.

The cost curve explains why:

AI-handled call: $0.40
Human-handled call: $7–$12

That's not a 10% improvement. That's a 95% cost reduction per interaction. At any meaningful call volume, the unit economics make the decision obvious.

But cost is the easy part of the story. The more interesting data is on quality.

AI-handled tickets CSAT: 4.10/5
Human-handled tickets CSAT: 4.30/5

That's a 0.20 point gap. Smaller than most people expected. And for routine interactions — balance inquiries, order status, basic troubleshooting — well-deployed voice AI is closing that gap to near zero.

First-year ROI on voice AI: 41%. By year 3: 124%. Average payback period: 3–6 months.

Three things I'm watching:

1. The tier 3 emergence. IVR replacement (tier 1) and autonomous resolution (tier 2) are well understood. The new frontier is agentic voice — multi-step actions, real-time data integration, proactive outreach. This doesn't exist at scale yet, but the production readiness is arriving.

2. The CSAT ceiling. 4.10 vs 4.30 sounds close, but the gap widens on complex cases. The companies seeing 4.30+ on AI channels are the ones who built their resolution flows around what AI does well, not just what it can technically handle.

3. The labor model shift. $80B in contact-center labor savings projected globally by Gartner by end of 2026. This isn't gradual displacement — it's restructuring in motion.

340% deployment growth in a year. The adoption curve is steep and it's still early.

The unit economics crossed the threshold. What's left is execution.
