31% of contact center agents say they're likely to quit within six months.

The headline gets spun as "workers fear AI replacement." The actual Verint data tells the opposite story.

The #1 driver of planned attrition: repetitive routine calls. Not AI anxiety. Not job insecurity. Agents are burning out because they spend 45% of every call — about three minutes per interaction — just searching for the right answer. Another three minutes gathering context that should have surfaced automatically at the start of the conversation.

Six minutes of avoidable friction, per call, across every call, every day. That's what's killing retention. Not AI.

Here's what the data from top-performing contact centers actually shows:

**AI reduces attrition by absorbing the work that drives attrition.**

The high-volume, low-complexity calls — the ones that hit 100+ times a day with zero variation — are the calls that burn agents out fastest. When voice AI handles that tier, human agents move up-market: complex issues, high-empathy conversations, cases where judgment actually matters. That's the work agents don't quit over.

AI-generated call summaries alone cut after-call work time by 35%. That's not a small quality-of-life improvement. After-call work is one of the most complained-about parts of the job — agents finish a difficult conversation and immediately have to write up the notes before the next call drops in. Removing 35% of that friction is measurable in engagement surveys.

The math on attrition is brutal. Call centers run 30-45% annual attrition. Each departing agent costs $10,000-$20,000 in replacement and ramp. For a 500-agent center at 35% attrition, that's $1.75M-$3.5M in annual replacement cost before any other variable.

AI that improves retention by 10 percentage points for that center: $350K-$700K in avoided costs annually. That's on top of the call handling unit economics.

Most AI in CX is pitched as headcount reduction. The smarter operators are tracking a different metric: agent retention rate, pre-AI vs. post-AI deployment.

The contact centers losing agents aren't losing them to AI. They're losing them to repetitive work that AI was built to handle.

Deploy AI to protect your human talent pipeline. The agents who stay are the ones who handle your hardest, highest-value customer interactions. That's worth more than the per-call cost savings.
