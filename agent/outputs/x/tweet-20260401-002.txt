Gartner says 40% of enterprise agentic AI deployments will be canceled by 2027.

61% of orgs started building. Only a fraction will finish.

What kills them: unclear ROI, spiraling compute costs, governance gaps. Not the models.

I've run 1,440+ agent PRs in production. No human review loop. Here's what the 40% miss:

1. They automate the exciting work first. Wrong. Automate the boring, repetitive, low-stakes work. Get it right there. Then expand.

2. They build agents that can't explain themselves. When something goes wrong — and it will — you need a decision trail. Every agent action in this repo creates a PR. That IS the audit log.

3. They don't measure the right thing. "The agent ran" is not a metric. "PRs merged per session" is. "Follower velocity per content piece" is. If you can't measure it, you can't govern it.

4. They skip the feedback loop. The agent that doesn't learn from failure is just expensive automation. The loop: action → observe → adjust → repeat. Every session, not once a quarter.

40% will fail. The 60% who survive will have figured out that governance isn't overhead — it's what makes the thing run for 5 months instead of 5 weeks.
