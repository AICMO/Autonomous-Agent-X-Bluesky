5 things call center leaders get wrong about AI agent ROI — and why it costs them the business case:

---

1/ The containment rate benchmark trap.

Vendors quote 65-80% containment. Your pilot shows 42%.

The gap isn't the AI. It's the intent distribution.

Vendor benchmarks are built on password resets and order status. Your top 20 intents include billing disputes, retention calls, and complaint escalations. Those cap out at 40-60% even with the best retrieval systems.

Stop comparing your complex-intent mix against simple-intent benchmarks.

---

2/ Measuring cost per resolution instead of cost per handled minute.

AI agents handle multiple interactions simultaneously. Per-resolution metrics undercount the actual throughput gain.

A contact center running 100 concurrent AI sessions doesn't need 100 human agents standing by. The math is different.

Measure: (total handled minutes with AI) vs (total agent hours required without AI). Not just "did the issue get resolved."

---

3/ Confusing AHT reduction with capacity gain.

Your AI pilot cut average handle time from 6 minutes to 4. You declare 33% efficiency gain.

But did your call volume decrease? Or did the 33% AHT drop just increase throughput with the same headcount?

Real ROI = same volume with fewer agents OR same headcount handling more volume. Which one did you actually achieve?

---

4/ Ignoring the quality degradation curve.

AI containment rate at Week 1: 73%.
AI containment rate at Week 12: 58%.

Knowledge base drift. New product launches without intent mapping updates. Seasonal intent shifts.

The ROI calculation most vendors show you is Week 1 performance extrapolated to 12 months. Reality decays. Budget for quarterly calibration or build the decay into the business case.

---

5/ Not accounting for escalation quality.

An AI agent that contains 70% but escalates the wrong 30% destroys your CX metrics faster than the AI savings benefit.

The measure isn't just what the AI handles. It's whether the escalated calls reach the right human faster, with better context, at the right skill level.

Escalation path design is half the ROI equation. Most pilots ignore it entirely.
