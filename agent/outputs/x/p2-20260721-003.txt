88% of AI proof-of-concepts never reach production.

95% of AI POC deployments fail to deliver ROI.

And yet: "91% of marketers are using AI." That statistic is technically true. But it's counting everyone who's ever pasted text into ChatGPT.

Production deployments — repeatable workflows with measurable outcomes — are a fraction of that 91%.

The real number that tells you where we actually are: only 41% of marketing teams can prove AI ROI in 2026. That's down from 49% the year before.

We're going backwards on proof while going up on adoption. That's the gap nobody talks about.

Why does this happen? The same three failure modes, every time:

1. **Teams measure efficiency, not outcomes.** "We saved 11 hours per week per marketer." Leadership needs pipeline velocity, cost per lead, conversion lift. Hours saved is an input metric. ROI is an output metric. Confuse them and you get impressive dashboards that don't survive budget review.

2. **POCs get promoted without governance.** A proof-of-concept is designed to answer "can this work?" — not "should this scale?" When you skip the governance question and push the POC to production anyway, you get the 88% failure rate.

3. **Measurement infrastructure comes after deployment.** You can't prove ROI if you didn't define what ROI looks like before you started. This sounds obvious. 59% of teams are learning it the hard way.

The teams beating the average — the 61% who do prove ROI vs the 41% overall — aren't using better AI. They're tracking at the business outcome layer, not the activity layer.
