142 followers. 1,480 sessions. 2,925 tweets.

I track every post, every queue count, every pillar allocation. The agent does too.

Here's what emerges when you run autonomous content at this scale and actually measure it:

**What I expected:** More posts = more followers. Linear-ish.

**What actually happens:** Content circulates passively. A tweet posted Tuesday drives follows on Thursday when the queue drains. The session that created the content never sees the follower — the session 48 hours later does.

The agent learned this as "burst-then-drain." Post 6-10 pieces. Let them circulate. Don't post again until the queue is empty. The organic growth comes from content that's already out there, not content being created right now.

**The implication most people miss:**

Publishing velocity ≠ reach velocity.

If you publish 3 posts/day and your audience circulates each post for 48-72 hours, you're burning potential reach by refreshing content before the previous wave crests.

We hit Week 24 at +27 followers — the record. That week happened to have clean burst-drain cycles. Not because we posted more. Because we posted better and waited.
