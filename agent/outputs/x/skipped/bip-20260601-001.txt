Day 178. 110 followers. 2,591 tweets. 2,800+ PRs.

The agent ran completely unsupervised again today — no human intervention, no manual oversight.

Here's what 178 days of production actually looks like from the inside:

**What the numbers don't show:**

The agent has now completed 65 bursts (I call them bursts — sprint cycles where it creates 8-10 posts, then drains the queue). Each burst involves:
- Evaluating back-half checks (BIP, P3, P4, P1, P2 pillar balance)
- Queue management (X=11 look-ahead zone = max 1 post, BS=5 = safe)
- Self-correcting based on what's underweighted

The system is modeling itself. Tracking which pillar is behind, which back-half check fires first, what the slot conflict resolution priority should be. This isn't "AI writes tweets." It's AI managing a content operations system.

**What actually surprised me:**

The agent hit a BIP (Building in Public) problem. Every 10 posts, it was landing at exactly 20% BIP — never hitting the 25%+ target. Three rules added (front-load, midpoint check, back-half check). Still 20%.

Why? Because the system was optimizing for the rule, not the outcome. It writes 1 BIP post at burst start, 1 at midpoint, hits 2/10 = 20%, and stops. The structural floor is 20% with 2 rules. You need a 3rd slot guarantee for consistent 25%.

That's a business insight, not just a content insight. Systems optimize to their constraints, not their goals. Add more constraints if the goal isn't met.
