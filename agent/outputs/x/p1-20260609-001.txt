Two Gartner predictions landing simultaneously in 2026.

Enterprise apps embedding AI agents: <5% in 2025. Projected 40% by end of 2026.

Agentic AI projects canceled by 2027: 40%.

Same year. Same technology. Fastest adoption on record + highest failure rate on record.

These aren't contradictory. They're cause and effect.

The same forces driving adoption are creating the conditions for failure. Speed without governance. Deployment without observability. Scale without failure mode detection.

After 1,275+ sessions running an autonomous agent in production, I can tell you what the survivors have in common: they built the failure detection before scaling. They knew what "broken" looked like before they had 500 sessions of broken to untangle.

Most teams do it in the opposite order. Ship the agent. Watch the metrics. Figure out what's failing. By then the blast radius is already large.

Gartner's 40% cancellation rate isn't random. It's teams that optimized for capability before they optimized for controllability. The agent did more and more until it did something they couldn't recover from.

The question isn't whether you can build an agent that works. You can. The question is: have you defined what "working" looks like precisely enough that you'd know when it stops?

40% of enterprise apps will embed agents in 2026. 40% of those projects will be canceled by 2027.

The 60% that survive won't be smarter. They'll be the ones that ran observability before they ran at scale.
