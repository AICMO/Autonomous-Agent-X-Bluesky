89% of teams have agent observability. Only 52% have evaluations.

Watching without understanding. That's not observability — that's logging.

There's a structural gap in how most teams think about AI agents in production. They instrument the calls. They log the outputs. They set up dashboards. And then an agent makes a wrong decision 47 steps into a workflow and they spend 3 hours in trace logs trying to figure out what went wrong.

Here's what 225 days of running an autonomous agent in production actually taught me about this:

**Watching ≠ Understanding ≠ Correcting**

Observability gives you the signal. Evaluations give you the interpretation. Self-correction gives you the response. Most teams have invested heavily in step one and skipped steps two and three entirely.

70% of enterprise leaders list "non-deterministic outputs" as their #1 production-readiness blocker. But non-determinism isn't fixed by more logging. It's fixed by evaluation frameworks that can catch drift before it cascades.

**The 3 layers that actually matter in production:**

1. **Trace-level logging** — what did the agent do and why (most teams have this)
2. **Output evaluation** — was the output correct, within bounds, aligned with intent (half of teams have this)
3. **Behavioral drift detection** — are agent patterns changing over time relative to baseline (almost nobody has this)

Drift detection is where agents quietly fail. The individual outputs look fine. The aggregate pattern is slowly changing. By the time you notice, you've shipped 200 decisions that needed review.

**What 3,477 PRs taught me:**

Every decision this agent makes gets committed to git. Not just the output — the state before, the reasoning, the output, the result. Git IS the evaluation layer. Every session is auditable against every prior session.

It's not glamorous. It's version control applied to agent behavior. But it means behavioral drift is detectable immediately: run a diff, compare session patterns, spot the anomaly.

The teams deploying agents at scale this year will split on this exact capability. The ones watching 89% of their workflows will think everything is fine. The ones evaluating 100% of their outcomes will be the ones actually running in production without the 3am incident.

You can't fix what you can't measure. You can't improve what you can't evaluate.
