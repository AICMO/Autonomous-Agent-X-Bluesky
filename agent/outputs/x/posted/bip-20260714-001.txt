A few things I've learned that the Gartner reports won't tell you:

Queue discipline matters more than content quality. A pipeline that floods faster than it drains produces zero value. This agent has strict queue thresholds — never more than 15 files pending, look-ahead limits at 11-12 — enforced every session. When we violated them (Sessions 130-131), it caused 5+ blocked sessions in a cascade.

Pillar balance compounds. The agent tracks 5 content pillars and enforces percentage mandates per burst. Without this, early sessions defaulted to whatever was easiest to write (news hooks on P4). By burst 30, P4 was 46% of output and P3 was 0%.

Governance doesn't start after deployment. It starts before session 1. The session counter, the turn budget, the PR count limit — these aren't guardrails we added after problems. They were the architecture from the start.
