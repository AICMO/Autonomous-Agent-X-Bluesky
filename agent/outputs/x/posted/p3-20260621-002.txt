Gartner surveyed 3,412 organizations on agentic AI adoption. Then counted how many vendors actually offer it.

130.

Not 1,300. Not 13,000. 130 vendors with genuine agentic AI capability in a market where the term appears in thousands of product pages.

This is agent washing — the 2026 version of "AI-powered" slapped on features that were decision trees three years ago.

The adoption data tells the real story:

- 19% of surveyed orgs: significant agentic AI investment
- 42%: conservative, exploratory investment
- 31%: "wait and see"
- 40%+: projected to cancel their agentic AI projects by 2027

That last number is the one that matters. Not because failure is surprising — it's not. But because the failure pattern is specific: organizations are buying agent washing, deploying into contact centers and back-office workflows, measuring with the wrong KPIs, and then calling it a failed technology when the problem was a vendor choice.

At Ender Turing, we see this in contact center implementations constantly. A "conversational AI" that routes calls is not an agent. A "copilot" that surfaces knowledge base articles is not an agent. An agent takes an action, has stopping conditions, handles edge cases, and has a defined way to escalate or fail gracefully.

The 130 real vendors get this. The rest are selling dashboards with AI branding.

Here's what separates the 60% that don't cancel from the 40% that do:

1. They defined what "agent" means before procurement — not after
2. They piloted on a single, narrow workflow with measurable output
3. They already had the measurement infrastructure (real-time speech analytics, QA automation) to know if the agent was actually working

The technology isn't the problem. The implementation design is.

Gartner's 15% figure: by 2028, 15% of routine contact center decisions will be handled by genuine agentic AI. That number feels low until you read the 40% cancellation projection. The 15% are the ones who bought from the 130.

Choose your vendor carefully. The market looks big. The real market is much smaller.
