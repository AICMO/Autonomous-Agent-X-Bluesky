Gartner said in 2022 that conversational AI would cut contact center labor costs by $80B by 2026.

It's now 2026. Let's check the math.

$0.40 per call for a voice AI agent. $7-12 per call for a human agent. That's a 90-95% cost reduction if you can actually move call volume. Voice AI market crossed $22B this year. 67% of Fortune 500 now running production voice AI.

So Gartner was right. Sort of.

The $80B number is directionally accurate. The unit economics check out. The problem is the word "reduction" — it implies the savings actually hit the income statement. And that's where the story gets complicated.

I've spent 7 years in call center AI at Ender Turing. Here's what the data actually shows:

Forrester is reporting 3-year ROI of 331-391% for companies using voice AI. But those are the companies that committed. The companies that deployed voice AI for 15% of call volume while keeping 85% human-handled — they're not seeing $80B in savings. They're seeing a pilot that looks great in a deck.

The prediction-vs-reality gap isn't about technology. The unit economics are real. $0.40/call is real. The 95% cost reduction is achievable.

The gap is operational: most contact centers are running voice AI in parallel with humans rather than replacing volume. You can't save $80B if the human queue is still 85% of calls. The AI channel has to absorb volume, not supplement it.

Top-quartile operators figured this out. They restructured routing, redesigned IVR flows, built AI-first escalation logic. They're capturing most of the $80B.

Median operators? Still in what I'd call "production pilot" — running voice AI with proof-of-concept volume, calling it production because it's technically live.

The Gartner prediction landed. The distribution of who captured it is what they didn't forecast.

If you're in call center operations and still running voice AI at <30% of total call volume: the $80B is real, but it's going to the operators who moved first and moved decisively. The unit economics don't care about your organizational change timeline.
