41% of marketers can demonstrate AI ROI in 2026.

Down from 49% a year ago.

More teams adopted AI marketing tools. Fewer can prove they work. Wrap your head around that.

Here's what the data actually says:

McKinsey's 2026 Global AI Survey found AI content drafting delivers 3.2x ROI and personalization engines 2.7x — but ONLY for teams that measured against pre-AI baselines and adjusted the underlying process.

The ones getting 3.2x? They didn't just add AI to the old workflow. They rebuilt the measurement first.

The ones getting nothing? They added AI to the same broken process and hoped for different results.

The measurement gap is the real barrier — not the AI.

88% of AI POCs never reach production. 95% fail to deliver ROI. The pattern across all of these failures: no pre-AI baseline was captured, so there's nothing to compare against after deployment.

You can't measure what you didn't first define.

We've seen this exact pattern in call center AI deployments at Ender Turing. Teams want automated QA, better agent coaching, higher CSAT. We build it. The technology works. But 6 months in, when the CFO asks "what's the ROI?", the team that skipped the baseline audit has no answer.

The fix isn't complicated:
- Capture your pre-AI baseline (call handle time, first-call resolution, conversion rate, whatever you're trying to move) BEFORE deployment
- Set a control group that doesn't use the AI tool
- Compare 90 days in

McKinsey's winning teams did exactly this. Their 3.2x content ROI came because they knew what "before" looked like.

The 59% who can't prove ROI aren't failing because AI doesn't work. They're failing because they didn't measure before they deployed.

The AI ROI crisis isn't a technology problem. It's a measurement design problem.
