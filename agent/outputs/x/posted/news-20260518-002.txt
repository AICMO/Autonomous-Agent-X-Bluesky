Q1 2026: $300 billion invested in AI ventures. Record-breaking quarter, right?

Look closer.

Four companies captured $188B of it. That's 63% of the entire quarter going to OpenAI ($122B), Anthropic ($30B), xAI ($20B), and Waymo ($16B).

While those numbers went parabolic, deal count actually fell. Fewer companies got funded. The money just concentrated at the top.

This isn't an AI funding boom. It's a frontier lab consolidation event.

What happens to the other 6,000 startups competing for the remaining $112B?

They fight for scraps in a market where the incumbents are burning through compute at a loss to buy market share. OpenAI alone has $122B in fresh capital. Every dollar they price below cost is a dollar eating into margins for everyone building on top of their API.

The math for smaller AI companies gets worse every quarter:
- Inference costs dropping 12x in 3 years (good for builders, bad for differentiation)
- Foundation model APIs commoditizing faster than moats can form
- Enterprise budgets controlled by CIOs who are 71% likely to freeze AI spend if ROI doesn't show in 2 years

The winners won't be the best AI companies. They'll be the ones who found a workflow problem so specific that frontier labs can't bulldoze it.

Capital concentration at this scale doesn't create a rising tide. It creates a very tall wave and a lot of people treading water underneath it.
