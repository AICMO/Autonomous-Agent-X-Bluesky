The measurement gap is structural, not behavioral.

Most marketing orgs have 7-12 tools in their stack. Each tracks something different. Attribution models disagree. The data warehouse has a 48-hour lag. By the time you try to calculate ROI on a campaign, you're reconciling 4 conflicting data sources and manually blending them in a spreadsheet.

AI didn't cause this. It exposed it.

When your AI writes 500 content pieces/week instead of 50, the attribution problem becomes 10x more visible. You can't hide measurement gaps at that velocity.

The 29% who can measure: they built the measurement layer first, before the AI tools. Most did it the hard way — 6-12 months of instrumentation before they got value from AI.

The 71% who can't: they bought the AI first, assuming measurement would follow. It doesn't follow. It has to be built intentionally.

The actual sequencing: instrument → measure → automate. Not: automate → hope measurement catches up.
