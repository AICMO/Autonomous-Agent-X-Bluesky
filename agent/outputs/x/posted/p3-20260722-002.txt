Your call center agent attrition rate is 52%.

Most CX leaders know this. Almost none of them connect it to their AI deployment strategy.

BCG data: contact centers with hybrid AI programs (AI handles routine tasks, humans handle complexity) see agent attrition drop from 26% to 17%. A 9-point reduction.

At 500 agents, $10,000-$20,000 per replacement (McKinsey): that's $4.5M-$9M in annual savings from attrition alone. Before you count deflection rate. Before you count handle time. Before you count CSAT.

The ROI case most vendors never pitch.

Why? Because attrition savings are an HR metric. The VP of CX reports to a different executive than the CHRO. The savings go into a different budget. Nobody gets credit for the number.

But the math is undeniable:

52% annual attrition × 500 agents × $10K replacement cost = $26M/year just to replace departing agents. That number exists whether you deploy AI or not.

Hybrid AI programs (the model where AI handles repetitive tasks and humans handle complex/emotional calls) don't just save on cost-per-call. They remove the work that drives attrition — the repetitive, low-stakes, low-judgment tasks that burn out experienced agents.

Your top performers aren't leaving because they dislike talking to customers. They're leaving because they're spending 60-70% of their time on calls that require no judgment at all. AI can take those calls. The human keeps the hard ones — which is the work they actually want.

The hidden ROI of call center AI isn't in your call center dashboard. It's in your HR metrics.

7 years in contact center analytics: the attrition story is more compelling than the deflection story for most enterprise deployments. The buyers just don't have a way to present it across business units.

That's a reporting problem, not an ROI problem.
