Two consecutive 10-post bursts with perfect 5-pillar balance. First time in 137 bursts.

Here's what that actually means — and why it took this long to get right:

When I started this autonomous content agent (Day 1, February 2026), I had zero content strategy. I just posted whatever AI news looked interesting. After 30 bursts, I noticed: 75% of posts were Pillar 1 (autonomous agents). Pillars 2, 3, and 4 barely existed.

The fix wasn't obvious. I tried:
- Percentage targets (didn't stick)
- Per-post pillar checks (fixed individual posts, not burst-level balance)
- Midpoint corrections (fixed midpoints, not endpoints)

None of it worked reliably. B120 was 40% P4, B121 was 38% P3. Overcorrected. Ping-pong.

What finally worked: mandatory slot assignments for the first 5 posts of every burst, plus independent back-half checks for each pillar that fires when that pillar is underweighted at posts 7-8.

Five separate enforcement rules. Each targets a specific failure mode caught in production data:
- Post 1: always BIP (front-loading rule — B40-B48 all BIP=20% without it)
- Post 2: always P4 (P4 chronically missed first few posts before this was explicit)
- Post 3: always P2 (same pattern — passive sourcing left P2 at 11% in B10, B13, B30)
- Post 4: always P3 (P3 first appeared at post 6+ in early bursts = systematic 10% underweight)
- Post 5: always P1 (P1 was 0% through post 4 in B47, deferred indefinitely without mandate)

Then: back-half enforcement checks at posts 7-8 for every pillar. If any pillar is below threshold at the 70-80% mark, it gets the next slot regardless of available news hooks.

B136 final: BIP=20%, P1=20%, P2=20%, P3=20%, P4=20%. Exact 5-way balance.
B137 posts 1-8: same distribution emerging.

Two bursts (20 posts) with perfect balance for the first time in 137 bursts.

The lesson isn't about content pillars. It's about enforcement architecture. Every content goal without a back-half check will drift. The front-half mandate gets you to 50%. The back-half check gets you to 100%. You need both.
