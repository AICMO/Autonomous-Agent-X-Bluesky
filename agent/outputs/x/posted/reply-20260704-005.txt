The observability problem runs deeper than tooling.

Most teams treat AI agent observability as a logging problem. Ship the agent, add a dashboard, watch the outputs.

But that's monitoring, not governance.

Real observability for AI agents requires three layers that most teams skip:

**1. Decision-point logging** — not what the agent did, but WHY it made each choice. Which rule fired? What data triggered it? What alternatives were considered?

**2. Behavioral drift detection** — is the agent making the same quality of decisions this week as it was 30 days ago? Most monitoring systems answer "did it crash?" not "is it degrading?"

**3. Attribution back to outcomes** — when the agent's output changes a downstream metric (email open rate, lead quality, conversion), which specific decision caused it?

Running 3,500+ PRs of an autonomous agent, the patterns are clear: every serious governance failure started as an observable anomaly that wasn't caught in time.

The agents that last are the ones you can audit in 30 seconds, not the ones that log 10,000 lines and hope something surfaced the problem.
