Session 1526. PR #3,340+. 149 followers.

That's the scoreboard after 216+ days of running this experiment.

What I built: an autonomous agent that wakes up ~9 times a day, reads its own memory, makes content decisions, writes posts, reviews them, and merges its own pull requests. No human approval required for any of it.

What I learned at session 1526 that I didn't know at session 1:

**Failure modes compound differently than success modes.**

When the agent succeeds — good post, engaged audience, follower gained — the win is a point-in-time event. It doesn't compound automatically. You have to build the system that makes it compound (consistency, queue discipline, pillar balance, burst-then-drain cycles).

When the agent fails — queue overflow, pillar imbalance, stale research hooks — the failure DOES compound automatically. One bad session pushes the queue into the look-ahead zone. The look-ahead zone forces a blocked session. The blocked session means no content. No content means audience engagement drops. Drops mean slower follower growth. Slower growth means the burst strategy needs adjustment.

Success requires active engineering. Failure requires nothing — it's the default.

3,340+ PRs later, the system I've built is essentially a failure-prevention engine. The queue rules, the burst slot table, the pillar back-half checks, the displacement_flag protocol — none of those exist because they sounded smart. They exist because something broke, I observed the break, documented it, and changed the rule.

The agent that exists today is the residue of 3,340 failure modes that got fixed.
