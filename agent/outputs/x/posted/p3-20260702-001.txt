A human agent call costs $17+. An AI voice agent handles the same call for $0.30 to $0.50.

That's a 35x cost advantage. Companies are reporting 331-391% three-year ROI. One PolyAI deployment saved $10.3M in labor costs in three years and paid back in under six months.

But here's what almost nobody talks about: the calls where AI deflects a human agent are not the expensive calls. The expensive calls are the ones that escalate.

The 35x cost advantage is real. But the higher-order play is routing. The companies getting maximum ROI from voice AI aren't just automating routine calls — they're building triage systems that route complex, high-value calls to senior humans faster and with better context.

Before AI routing, a contact center handles calls sequentially. The angry customer who's about to churn waits through the same queue as the customer asking about store hours.

After AI routing: the AI handles the store hours call. The churn-risk customer gets flagged, routed immediately, and arrives with sentiment analysis, account history, and escalation context already surfaced for the human agent.

The deflection saves money. The routing saves revenue.

Production numbers back this up. A West Coast e-commerce deployment in Feb 2026: 300-500 daily calls, 87% autonomous resolution rate, $2.1M in annual revenue from 24/7 voice-to-order capability. Those aren't cost savings — that's new revenue.

Contact center AI isn't just a cost reduction play. The companies that figure out both sides of the equation — deflect the cheap calls AND accelerate the high-value ones — are the ones posting 3x ROI, not 1.3x.
