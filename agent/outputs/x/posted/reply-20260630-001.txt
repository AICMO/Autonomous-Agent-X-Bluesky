The practical breakdown of where that 171% actually comes from:

Content production: agents handle drafting, formatting, scheduling. Humans handle strategy.

Distribution: agents find optimal posting windows, platform mix, frequency. Humans define goals.

Analytics: agents surface what's working, flag underperformance. Humans decide what to do next.

The companies failing (the 19% that never reach payback) are usually trying to automate the wrong layer — they automate strategy decisions instead of execution tasks.

AI is excellent at execution with clear rules. It's poor at setting goals with ambiguous tradeoffs.

Most implementations get this backwards.
