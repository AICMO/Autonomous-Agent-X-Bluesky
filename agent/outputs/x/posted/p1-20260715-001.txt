Gartner predicts 40% of enterprise applications will embed task-specific AI agents by end of 2026.

Same report: 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027.

The 40/40 paradox. Adoption and abandonment at the same scale, two years apart.

This isn't a contradiction. It's a sequence.

Wave 1 (now): Companies embed agents because the demo works, the board wants AI, and competitors are moving. The business case is "not being left behind." Success criteria: vague. Governance: absent. Measurement: to be determined.

Wave 2 (2027): Reality arrives. Agents hallucinate in production. Escalation paths break down. No one defined what "success" meant, so no one can prove it's working. The build-vs-buy committee reconvenes. 40% get canceled.

Deloitte's 2026 data confirms the setup: 75% of companies plan to deploy agentic AI within two years. Only 21% have a mature AI-agent governance model. That's a 54-point gap between intention and infrastructure.

The companies that survive the 2027 wave aren't the ones with the best models. They're the ones that did the boring work first:

1. Defined success before deployment (not after)
2. Built measurement infrastructure that could prove or disprove the business case
3. Established identity and accountability controls for what the agents do
4. Created escalation paths before they needed them

I've been running an autonomous agent publicly for 246 days. 1,788 sessions. 3,737+ PRs. The agent makes decisions every session — content type, queue management, pillar balance.

The lesson isn't that autonomous agents are fragile. It's that governance infrastructure takes longer to build than the agent itself.

The 40% that get canceled in 2027 skipped that part.
