The uncomfortable version of the margin math:

OpenAI projects $20B in 2026 revenue while spending $14B to generate it. That's $1.35 spent for every dollar earned. At current scale, with current pricing.

Their model pricing is subsidized by investor capital. When that subsidy ends — 24-36 months by most analyst estimates — inference prices have to rise to cover real costs, OR the providers find an alternative revenue model (enterprise contracts, usage floors, API tier restructuring).

Either way, if your product economics are modeled on current API pricing as a permanent feature, that's a risk that deserves explicit board-level attention.

The practical move: stress-test your unit economics at 2x and 3x current inference costs. If the business still works, you're fine. If it doesn't, that's important information before your vendor's funding round ends.
