Token prices dropped 280x in two years.

Enterprise AI spend rose 320% in the same period.

This is Jevons Paradox playing out in real time. When something gets cheaper, you use more of it. Much more. The math doesn't add up the way people expect.

Here's what's actually happening in enterprise AI economics right now:

**The token price paradox**
Costs fell dramatically. But agentic AI workflows now trigger 10-20 LLM calls per user task. RAG architectures inflate context windows 3-5x. Agentic models require 5-30x more tokens per task than standard chatbots (Gartner, March 2026).

So you saved 99% per token. And spent 1,000% more tokens.

Net result: your AI bill is higher than ever.

**Where this breaks for startups**
If you built your pricing model on traditional token economics, you're in trouble. A customer paying $20/month might generate $18-25 in inference costs during heavy reasoning tasks. The unit economics simply don't work.

OpenAI spent $1.35 for every dollar it earned. They have $40B in funding to absorb that. Most startups don't.

**The governance layer nobody's pricing**
It gets worse. The EU AI Act's high-risk system obligations take full effect in 2026. Penalties up to €35M or 7% of global revenue. Most enterprise AI deployments have no audit trails, no explainability, no compliance layer.

The "inference cost" is just the compute bill. The real cost includes governance infrastructure, security reviews, legal exposure, and incident response.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 3,285+ PRs now. The hidden operational costs — monitoring, state management, failure recovery — are 3-4x what the pure compute costs suggest.

**What this means for the next 18 months**
- Race-to-zero pricing is VC-funded. It won't last.
- Startups that survive will have unit economics that account for agentic token consumption.
- Governance costs are now a line item, not an afterthought.

Gartner predicts inference costs drop another 90% by 2030. But by then, agentic workflows will consume 100x more tokens per task.

The floor keeps dropping. The ceiling keeps rising.

Build accordingly.
