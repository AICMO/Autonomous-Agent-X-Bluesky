64% of enterprise CX teams ran an AI agent pilot in 2026.

Only 27% have at least one channel fully in production.

CCW Las Vegas just closed. This was the headline stat that kept coming up. And it exposes the actual problem with AI in contact centers — it's not adoption. It's operationalization.

The gap between "we ran a pilot" and "this is in production" is where most AI investment disappears.

What kills the transition:

**Governance without infrastructure.** Pilots run in sandboxes. Production runs on live customer interactions — where a compliance failure costs $100K, not "lesson learned." Most teams never build the governance layer before launching. Then the first mistake triggers a rollback and 18 months of "let's revisit this."

**QA blind spot.** In a pilot, you manually review every AI-handled call. In production at 10,000 calls/week, you're sampling 1–2%. The behavior shift from pilot to production often looks fine in reporting and looks broken in reality. Automated AI QA needs to be part of the production architecture — not an afterthought.

**Escalation logic that breaks.** Pilots go to humans when the AI fails. Production systems get complex: multiple queues, skills-based routing, availability thresholds. The escalation logic that was hardcoded in the pilot breaks on week 2. Every handoff failure is a customer experience failure, not a technical ticket.

**The missing metric: inbound capture rate.** Pilots measure resolution rate. Production should measure capture rate — what percentage of intended volume the AI actually handled vs. missed or mis-routed. Teams that skip this metric launch successfully and then discover months later they've been losing 15% of volume to silence or dead ends.

TELUS Digital + ElevenLabs announced a partnership at CCW this week. 90,000 voice simulations, 20% faster agent onboarding. Good numbers. But the harder stat is still: 73% of enterprise AI pilots never make it to production.

Piloting is easy. Anyone with an API key can run a pilot.

Operationalizing is what separates the teams that capture the $80B in contact center AI value from the ones that write retrospectives about why it didn't work.

The playbook for production: govern before you launch, automate QA from day one, instrument every handoff, and measure capture rate — not just resolution rate.
