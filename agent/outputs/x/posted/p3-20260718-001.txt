$0.40 per AI call vs $7-12 per human agent.

That's the number contact center CFOs are working with in 2026. And it's creating a completely new problem: the math is too good.

When the unit economics are that lopsided, pressure to cut human headcount happens before the infrastructure is ready to handle it. I've seen this pattern at Ender Turing — organizations rush to declare "we're 70% automated" while the remaining 30% of interactions (billing disputes, retention calls, complex escalations) are exactly the calls that destroy CX if mishandled.

The $0.40 vs $12 comparison is real. But it masks the attrition cost equation that nobody puts in the same spreadsheet.

BCG published data this year: contact centers running hybrid AI programs had 17% annual agent attrition. All-human programs: 26%.

That 9-point difference on a 100-agent floor is $500K-900K in annual savings from NOT replacing agents as fast. Burnout-driven turnover is costing the industry $10K-21K per replacement. At 30-45% annual attrition rates (some centers hit 60%), you're replacing a third of your workforce every year.

The AI value case that most operators present:
→ Cost per call reduction
→ AHT reduction
→ Containment rate improvement

The AI value case they're missing:
→ Attrition reduction (BCG: -9pp in hybrid programs)
→ Replacement cost avoidance ($10K-21K per agent)
→ Knowledge preservation (tenured agents staying longer vs churning)

Run the full model. $0.40 per call is a headline. The real ROI is in what doesn't happen: the agent who doesn't burn out, doesn't quit, doesn't need replacing.
