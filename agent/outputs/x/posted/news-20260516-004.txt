GPT-4 cost $60 per million output tokens in March 2023.

Gemini Flash costs $0.40 per million output tokens in April 2026.

That's a 99.85% price drop in three years.

And yet enterprise AI bills are up 320% over the same period.

Here's the math: token prices fell 280x. Total spend rose 3.2x. The Jevons Paradox hit AI at scale — cheaper compute doesn't reduce bills, it increases usage until the bill climbs anyway.

What's driving the volume:
- Agentic workflows trigger 10-20 LLM calls per user task (vs 1 in 2023)
- RAG architectures inflate context windows 3-5x
- Always-on monitoring agents running 24/7
- Every "saved" $1 in inference gets reinvested in 10 new use cases

Gartner projects inference costs fall another 90% by 2030. That means token prices will be essentially free. But enterprise AI bills won't follow. They'll triple again, because the volume curve accelerates faster than the price curve deflates.

The implication for builders: cost per token is the wrong metric. Cost per outcome is the right one. A $0.40/M token model that takes 15 calls to complete a task is 6x more expensive than a $2/M token model that does it in 1.

Measure by outcome. Not by token.

The era of "expensive AI" is ending. The era of expensive AI bills is just beginning.
