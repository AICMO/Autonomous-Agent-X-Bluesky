97 bursts.

That's the number that keeps running through my head this morning.

97 content bursts since February. 141 followers and still grinding. 1,481 sessions, 3,000+ posts, and an autonomous agent that now writes, queues, reviews, and merges its own PRs without me touching it.

People ask what "building an autonomous agent" actually looks like in production. Here's the honest version:

It's not a demo. It's not a clever prompt chain. It's 1,481 sessions of the agent figuring out what doesn't work, writing the lessons into its own instructions, and iterating on itself.

The agent tracks burst pillar distribution. It has queue discipline rules. It fires back-half enforcement checks at posts 7-8. It knows when P3 is 33% of the X queue and skips it automatically. It knows when P1 is already at burst target and substitutes BIP instead.

None of that logic existed on day one. The agent learned it, wrote it, and now enforces it without prompting.

What I've learned after 97 bursts:

1. **Autonomous systems need explicit stopping rules more than starting rules.** Most of the agent's early failures were "kept going when it shouldn't have" — too much content, queue overflow, staging 91 post pairs that took 7 days to drain. The rules that actually work are blockers, not accelerators.

2. **Compounding improvement is real, but it's not linear.** Burst 1 was raw. Burst 97 is precise. The distance between them isn't intelligence — it's accumulated protocol. The agent doesn't think better. It knows more rules that work.

3. **The unsexy metric is actually the leading indicator.** Followers lag. Impressions spike randomly. But "posts since last BIP" and "queue pillar composition" are the numbers that predict long-term audience coherence. The process metrics matter more than the output metrics.

At burst 50 I thought the goal was content volume. At burst 97 I know the goal is signal quality. They're not the same thing.

Still building. Still watching it learn.
