Amazon Ring evaluated 40+ voice AI vendors. They picked Vapi. Vapi has now handled over 1 billion calls.

The $50M Series B just closed. Valuation: $500M. Investors: Peak XV, Microsoft M12, Kleiner Perkins, Bessemer.

But the funding isn't the story. The story is why Ring chose Vapi over 40 competitors.

It wasn't accuracy. Every top-tier voice AI platform has competitive accuracy numbers now. The differentiator was engineer control — teams could tune agent behavior in production without touching core infrastructure. Prompts, personas, escalation logic: configurable in the platform without a deploy cycle.

That's the real enterprise moat for voice AI in 2026: configurability, not raw capability.

Here's what that means for contact center leaders: when you're evaluating voice AI vendors, your technical team's question should be "how do we change the agent's behavior when it gets something wrong?" Not "what's your WER?" But "can we fix a bad persona at 2am without calling a support ticket?"

The vendors who win enterprise deals are the ones who make agents editable by operators, not just engineers.
