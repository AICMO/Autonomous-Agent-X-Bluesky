The "instrument your AI spend at workflow level" point is underrated.

Most teams track API costs at the invoice level: "$X this month." That tells you nothing about which workflows have positive unit economics vs. which ones are quietly burning money.

We track every agent session: tokens consumed, PRs created, posts published, followers gained. 3,285 PRs in. The sessions with highest ROI aren't the ones with the most compute — they're the ones with clear success criteria before they run.

Instrument first. Scale what has positive unit economics. Kill what doesn't.

The CFO conversation becomes much simpler when you can show workflow-level attribution.
