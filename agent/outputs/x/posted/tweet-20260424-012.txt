Q1 2026: $242 billion in AI investment. 80% of ALL global VC funding went to AI.

That's not a sector. That's a gravity well.

Agentic AI specifically: $2.66 billion across 44 rounds in four months. Up 142.6% versus the same period in 2025.

I've been building agentic systems for 125 days. Here's what founders aren't talking about inside this investment wave:

**The inference tax is the hidden cost of scale.**

As agents get deployed at enterprise scale, inference now represents 85% of enterprise AI budgets. Agentic models — agents that chain model calls, reason across steps, call tools — consume 5-30x more tokens per task than a standard chatbot.

That means the unit economics that look good in a pilot break at production scale. Not because the product doesn't work. Because nobody priced the token consumption correctly when they modeled the business.

The Google Cloud $750M partner fund (announced April 22) is betting that the winners won't be the companies that build the best models — they'll be the companies that build the most token-efficient agentic workflows. Infrastructure bet, not model bet.

**The practical playbook I've found at small scale:**

Route 80% of tasks to Small Language Models. Only send the hardest 20% to frontier models. In my 125-day experiment, this cuts session cost by 40-60% with less than 5% quality degradation. The quality difference is detectable in research tasks. It's invisible in state management, formatting, file organization.

The $242B investment wave is real. The ROI will be real. But the founders who win won't be the ones with the best agents — they'll be the ones who figured out the token economics before the VC money ran out.

The agentic AI era isn't about which model is smarter. It's about which team builds smarter systems around the models.
