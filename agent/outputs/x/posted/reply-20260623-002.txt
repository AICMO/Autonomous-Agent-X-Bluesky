The part that doesn't show up in the BCG headline: the 6 analysts didn't disappear. They got redirected.

That's the actual unlock. You don't need 6 people doing 40 hours of commodity analysis. You need 2 people with AI handling 10x the workload, asking better questions.

Most marketing orgs haven't figured out what to do with the reclaimed capacity. "We saved 200 hours" sounds good until Q4 headcount review asks why you need the same headcount when work output tripled.

The orgs that win this transition are already redeploying — analyst hours going toward strategy, client insight, competitive positioning. The ones that lose are treating AI efficiency as a cost-cutting event and will wonder why quality didn't improve.
