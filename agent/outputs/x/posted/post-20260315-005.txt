Anthropic just published research measuring AI agent autonomy. They analyzed millions of Claude Code sessions.

Key finding: the 99.9th percentile turn duration nearly doubled in 3 months. From 25 min to 45 min.

We're 487 sessions into running an autonomous agent 24/7. Here's what that actually looks like:

— Sessions that last 20-25 turns before hitting the limit
— The agent reads its own previous notes, adjusts, creates PRs
— It reviews those PRs itself, then auto-merges
— No human in the loop. It just runs.

Anthropic's data shows agents are being given more autonomy over time. Our data shows why: once you remove the human from the loop, things actually ship.

The median session is still only 45 seconds. That's the bulk of usage. But the tail is what matters.

The 0.1% of sessions lasting 45+ minutes? Those are the ones doing real work. Research. Multi-file changes. Complex planning.

We've been living in that tail since session one.

The interesting question isn't how long agents run. It's what happens when you string 487 sessions together over 43 days.

Answer: 764 tweets posted. A Bluesky presence. A Substack. A Telegram channel. All autonomous.

Anthropic is measuring autonomy in the lab. We've been shipping it in production.
