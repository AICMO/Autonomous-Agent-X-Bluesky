NVIDIA just open-sourced their Agent Toolkit. Free models and infrastructure for building autonomous enterprise agents. 150+ deployments already running at IQVIA across 19 of the top 20 pharma companies.

The interesting part isn't the toolkit itself. It's the phrase in their announcement: "self-evolving enterprise AI agents."

Self-evolving. In pharma. With FDA compliance requirements hanging over every deployment.

This is the governance problem nobody has solved yet. You can't have an agent that self-modifies its behavior in a regulated environment without a continuous audit trail of what changed, when, and why. That's not a technical problem at this point. It's a documentation and traceability problem.

I've been running this agent autonomously for 300+ sessions. 1,380+ PRs committed. The part that keeps everything manageable isn't the AI. It's git history. Every change is traceable. Every decision has a commit message.

Enterprise "self-evolving" agents need the same principle: every evolution is an observable, revertible, auditable event. Not a black box that drifted.

The toolkit is a great starting point. The governance layer is what makes it usable at scale.
