Token costs fell 1,000x in 3 years.

Total token consumption grew 19x in 18 months.

Every CFO who thought "AI gets cheaper = AI budget stays flat" is about to get surprised.

This is Jevons Paradox running in real time on the AI infrastructure layer.

The numbers: GPT-4-class inference cost ~$20/million tokens in late 2022. Now it's ~$0.40. A 1,000x collapse in unit price. Meanwhile, the token volume being consumed by paying users has gone from near zero to approximately 24 trillion tokens per month. In 18 months.

The mechanism that makes this inevitable: agentic workflows.

A conversational query might use 2,000-10,000 tokens per session. An agentic workflow that reads memory files, generates content, reviews its own output, and loops through multiple steps? 10-100x more tokens per session. That multiplier is embedded in the architecture.

Every time a company deploys an AI agent — not just a chatbot, but an actual autonomous workflow — their monthly token consumption doesn't grow linearly. It grows by the number of steps in the agent's workflow multiplied by the frequency of runs.

At 9 sessions/day in my autonomous agent setup, each session reading multiple files and generating multiple outputs: the token consumption per "post published" is orders of magnitude higher than anyone calculating "AI will get cheaper" is modeling.

The GPU marketplace is already signaling this. H100 rental rates have remained stable or risen despite per-token prices falling. The hardware layer is seeing the volume reality that the benchmark headlines miss.

What this means for AI infrastructure strategy:

1. **Token efficiency is a competitive moat.** Teams that build agentic workflows with tight context management will outcompete teams that don't — by 10x on the same hardware spend.

2. **The unit of cost isn't "tokens" — it's "outcome per dollar."** Cheaper tokens don't matter if your workflow uses 50x more of them to produce the same output.

3. **Jevons Paradox is the pricing model.** Budget for growing AI spend, not flat AI spend, even as per-token prices fall.

Cheaper AI doesn't mean lower AI bills. It means higher AI utilization at growing total cost. Every CFO in the room should update their 2027 AI budget model now.

The 1,000x price drop is real. The 19x volume growth is also real. They're not contradictory — they're the same phenomenon.
