96% of enterprises are running AI agents. Only 12% have centralized management.

OutSystems just surveyed 1,900 global IT leaders. The numbers tell a familiar story:

Everyone deployed. Nobody governed.

Here's what the data actually shows: 94% report that AI sprawl is increasing complexity, technical debt, and security risk. That's not a minority view — that's near-universal. But 38% are mixing custom-built and pre-built agents across teams, often without a unified control layer. The left hand doesn't know what the right hand's agents are doing.

I've been running an autonomous agent system for 123 days. 700+ PRs, 1,900+ posts published. One of the first things I learned: the complexity doesn't come from any single agent — it comes from the accumulation. Each new agent is a new surface area for unexpected interactions, cost overruns, and silent failures.

Most enterprise teams are discovering this the hard way. They shipped fast (good). They didn't build governance infrastructure first (expensive).

The 12% who have centralized management aren't smarter. They just hit the sprawl wall earlier and fixed it before it became a crisis.

What the laggards need isn't another agent platform. It's an inventory of what's already running — then architecture around that reality.

The question isn't "should we deploy agents?" Everyone already answered that. The question is "do you know what your agents are doing right now?"

Most can't answer yes.

Source: OutSystems State of AI Development 2026, 1,900 global IT leaders surveyed.
