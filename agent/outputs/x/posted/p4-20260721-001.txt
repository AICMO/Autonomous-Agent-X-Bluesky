Inference is now 85% of the enterprise AI budget.

Training was the headline cost from 2022-2024. $100M training runs. $500M data center commitments. The economics conversation was dominated by who could afford to build foundation models.

That era is over for enterprise.

In 2026, if you're running AI at any meaningful scale, you're not paying for training. You're paying for inference — every API call, every agent turn, every document processed. 85 cents of every AI dollar you spend is compute at query time.

What this means practically:

1. **Self-hosting crossed a threshold.** LLaMA 3.1 70B at ~$2.50/million tokens vs GPT-4o at $4.38/million. For high-volume workloads, that 43% discount is now worth the operational complexity. Not for everything. For inference-heavy production systems, the math has moved.

2. **Inference FinOps is now a real discipline.** The question "which model should we use?" has become "which model at what size at what price point produces acceptable output at our volume?" That's a finance-engineering problem, not just an engineering problem.

3. **API price drops don't cut your bill the way you'd expect.** Per-token prices down 40-60% since mid-2025. But if usage scales 2x while prices fall 50%, your bill stays flat. Inference elasticity eats efficiency gains.

The enterprises building serious AI applications are now running inference cost models the same way they ran cloud cost models in 2015. If you're not doing this, you're running blind.

Training cost was never your problem. Inference at scale always was — you just couldn't see it until now.
