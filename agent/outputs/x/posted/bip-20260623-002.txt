96 bursts into autonomous publishing. Here's what the data says about content rhythm that nobody talks about.

The prevailing advice: post consistently. Every day. Same time. Algorithm loves routine.

What actually works when you have 1,479 sessions of data: burst-then-drain.

Here's the pattern my autonomous agent discovered after getting it wrong for 8 consecutive bursts:

**The failure mode (weeks 1-8):**
Create 2-3 posts/session. Every session. Queue stays at 8-12 constantly. Zero drain gaps. Zero follower spikes.

Result: 91 queued pairs accumulated over 7 days. 13 consecutive blocked sessions. 1.1MB of research files that never became posts. Zero engagement growth during that period.

**What changed:**
Session 130: Added hard queue limits (max 15). Forced drain cycles.

The insight: followers don't arrive when you post. They arrive when content circulates without new content competing for attention.

Burst → fill queue to 12-13 → stop creating → let it drain completely → burst again.

During the drain phase, X's algorithm keeps serving your recent posts. New posts during drain would cannibalize that circulation.

**The numbers that convinced me:**

B23 burst (March 2026): Created 13 posts in 2 sessions. Queue 0→13. Then blocked for 4 sessions while it drained.

Follower additions happened in sessions 3-6 of the drain period — NOT during creation.

Publishing velocity is a vanity metric. Drain velocity is what actually matters.

**What this means for enterprise content teams:**

Your content calendar is probably wrong. You're optimizing for publishing consistency when you should be optimizing for circulation windows.

Post less. Let each piece breathe. Measure engagement velocity on day 3-7 post-publish, not day 1.

The burst-drain cycle also prevents your own team from drowning in content ops. Spikes of creation followed by deliberate silence.

96 bursts. Each one teaching the same lesson more clearly.
