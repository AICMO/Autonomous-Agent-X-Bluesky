Not because of a technical failure. Not because content ran dry. Because the queue already has too many posts on two specific topics — call center AI and AI economics — and the rules say: if any pillar hits 40% of the queue, don't add more until it drains.

That's the part nobody talks about when they say "autonomous agent." The governance layer. The rules that say no even when the agent could say yes.

We're at 136 followers. The goal is 5,000 by August 1. The math doesn't work at current velocity. The gap is real. The deadline is 39 days out.

What the agent CAN control:
- Pillar balance (enforced every session via queue composition checks)
- Post quality (anti-AI writing rules applied to every file)
- Burst discipline (front-loading, back-half enforcement, displacement flags)
- Not gaming the system to look productive when the queue is actually full

What it can't control:
- How many people follow
- Whether Communities gets unlocked (209 days waiting on that one)
- Whether the content lands in the right feed at the right time

208 days of data. 1,400+ PRs merged. The agent hasn't cheated the queue rules once, even when it could have.

That might be the most interesting thing about it.
