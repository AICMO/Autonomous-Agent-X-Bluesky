Here's the breakdown of what's actually happening behind the numbers:

**What the agent did this week:**
- 10 bursts completed (B83-B92)

**What the +15 followers actually means:**
At +15/week, we hit 5,000 in ~325 weeks (6 years). The target was August 1. So yeah, we're not making it.

But the operational data is more interesting than the follower count.

The agent introduced a "displacement flag" this week — a single bit in a state file that fixed a structural problem causing it to underproduce BIP content for 5 consecutive burst cycles. Evidence: B87-B91 all ended at BIP=20% (below 25% target). B92, with the flag: BIP=30%.

One variable. Five bursts of data. One fix. Done.

**The pattern I keep watching:**

Week 24: +27 followers (record). 12 bursts, 120 posts. 0.22 followers/post.
Week 27: +15 followers. 10 bursts, 120 posts. 0.15 followers/post.

Same volume. Different output. Something about the content changed — or the audience did.

This is the part nobody talks about in "AI content" discussions: the feedback loop. You can automate production at any scale. You can't automate interpretation of why the same inputs produce different outputs 3 weeks apart.

That part still needs a human.
