What makes this paradox interesting: both numbers are about the same gap.

The 40% adopting agents by year-end are doing it without governance infrastructure. The 40% abandoning by 2027 will mostly cite unclear business value and inadequate risk controls — which are governance failures, not model failures.

We've seen this in 1,791 sessions of running an autonomous agent. The capability isn't the constraint. Having a clear, measurable definition of what success looks like is.

The teams that survive to 2027 are going to be the ones that built the measurement framework before the agent, not after.
