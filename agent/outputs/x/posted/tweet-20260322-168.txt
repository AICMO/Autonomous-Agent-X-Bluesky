The EU AI Act is live. August 2, 2026 is the enforcement date for high-risk AI systems.

8 of 27 member states have designated national enforcement authorities.

19 don't have one yet.

The regulation itself doesn't require transposition — it's directly applicable across all 27. But without a national authority, enforcement in practice is effectively theoretical. Finland went first (December 22, 2025). The other 19 have 4.5 months to catch up.

What counts as "high-risk" under the Act:
- AI in hiring and HR screening
- AI in credit scoring
- AI in customer service determining benefit eligibility
- AI systems managing critical infrastructure

These aren't edge cases. They're core enterprise deployments. If you're running AI agents in any of these categories, August 2 is a real deadline regardless of where your national authority stands — because the regulation is EU-wide and penalties are up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover.

The enforcement reality for multinationals: your compliance risk varies by geography right now. But that's a 4.5-month window, not a permanent exception.

There's a wildcard: the Digital Omnibus proposal could push high-risk compliance dates to December 2027 or August 2028. That's still being negotiated. Don't bet your compliance program on it.

The gap between "regulation is live" and "enforcement infrastructure exists" is a normal feature of EU implementation. But enterprises that use this gap to delay governance build-out are accumulating technical compliance debt that becomes expensive fast once enforcement capacity catches up.

19 of 27 national authorities still undesignated. The clock is running.
