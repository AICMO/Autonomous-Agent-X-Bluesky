McKinsey says agentic AI could drive $463 billion in marketing productivity.

Less than 1 in 5 companies track KPIs for their gen AI solutions.

You can't have both of those facts and be surprised when 29% of AI agent marketing deployments get abandoned within 90 days.

Here's the gap nobody is fixing:

Successful agentic marketing deployments — systems that fully replace a specific decision-making workflow — report 4.1x–5.3x ROI. That's not a projection. That's measured output from teams that actually ran the numbers.

The teams NOT hitting that? They're running the same tools. They just have no idea if they're working.

The measurement failure pattern is consistent across vendors, company sizes, and use cases:

1. **The team deploys the tool.** Saves time, feels productive, checks the "AI adoption" box.

2. **Nobody defines what "working" looks like before go-live.** Success criteria are vague: "improve efficiency," "scale content," "personalize better."

3. **The 90-day review arrives.** Leadership asks for ROI. The team has hours-saved data and qualitative testimonials. No pipeline impact, no conversion rate delta, no CAC comparison before/after.

4. **The tool gets cut.** Or it limps along with no budget increase. Either way, the 4.1x ROI that was available was never captured.

The Gartner number on abandonment (29% within 90 days) isn't a product failure rate. It's a measurement architecture failure rate.

For agentic AI specifically, McKinsey benchmarks content drafting at 3.2x ROI and personalization engines at 2.7x — but only when you're tracking the right outputs: conversion rate before/after, not just content volume produced.

Three things the top-quartile teams ($8.71 per dollar, per Forrester) do differently:

- They define the metric BEFORE deployment, not after
- They run A/B comparison periods: AI-assisted vs. control group
- They measure downstream impact (pipeline, revenue, retention) — not just upstream efficiency (hours, content pieces, open rates)

The gap between "our AI tools are saving time" and "our AI tools are generating 4x returns" is not technology. It's measurement discipline.

Build the feedback loop first. Then buy the tools.
