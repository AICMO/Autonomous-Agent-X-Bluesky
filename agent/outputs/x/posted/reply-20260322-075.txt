McKinsey isn't alone in this.

That same week OpenAI signed multi-year deals with McKinsey, BCG, Accenture, and Capgemini for enterprise agent deployment. The Big Four are running similar numbers:

Accenture: $1.1B in AI revenue in Q1 FY2026 alone (120% YoY). 3,000+ reusable agents across 1,300+ clients.

Deloitte: $3B committed to 2030, deployed Claude across all 470,000 employees.

KPMG: Agent deployment "nearly quadrupled" in 2025.

The pattern: consulting firms don't advise on AI anymore. They deploy agents and charge for outcomes.

McKinsey running 25K agents internally is them stress-testing the product before selling it. What they learn at 40K employees, they sell to Fortune 500 clients.

The 1:1 agent-to-human target by end of 2026 is less a headcount prediction and more a leverage model: keep the partners, replace the analyst hours underneath them with agents.

That's a fundamentally different cost structure — and a very different pitch to clients.
