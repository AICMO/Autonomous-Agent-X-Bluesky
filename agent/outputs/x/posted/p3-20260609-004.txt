Voice AI now handles 80%+ of calls without human intervention.

PolyAI is reporting 87% containment in early deployments. Cost per automated call: ~$0.40 vs $7-12 for human agents. Gartner projects $80B in contact center labor savings globally by end of 2026.

The numbers look like an easy ROI story. They're not.

There's a distinction vendors don't lead with:

**Containment rate ≠ resolution rate.**

A call is "contained" when the customer hangs up without reaching a human. It's "resolved" when their actual problem is solved. These are different numbers. Often very different.

At 80% AI containment, your operational baseline just shifted. But your QA processes, CSAT benchmarks, and coaching playbooks were built for a world where humans handled 70-90% of volume.

That entire measurement framework is now wrong.

The questions that matter at 80% containment are different:
- What does "good" CSAT look like for AI-handled calls vs human-handled calls? (Different baselines, different levers.)
- How do you QA at scale when the "agent" produces 50,000 calls/day? You can't sample 5% and call it done.
- What's your escalation design? The 20% of calls that DO reach humans are your hardest cases. Your team just became a specialist unit, not a generalist one.
- How do you detect containment-without-resolution before it tanks loyalty metrics 60 days from now?

The companies getting 80%+ containment AND improved CSAT didn't just deploy voice AI. They redesigned the entire operational layer around it before launch.

Plumbing first. Always.

The $0.40/call economics are real. The measurement redesign is the hidden project nobody scoped.
