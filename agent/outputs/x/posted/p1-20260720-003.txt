Everyone is scrambling for EU AI Act compliance on August 2.

Most of them are scrambling for the wrong deadline.

Here's what actually activates August 2 vs what got pushed to December 2027:

**What's live now (August 2, 2026):**
- Article 50 transparency obligations: if you deploy a chatbot, users must know it's AI
- Deepfake and synthetic media labeling: mandatory disclosure on AI-generated content
- GPAI enforcement: General Purpose AI model providers (think: companies building on frontier models at scale) are now subject to systemic risk protocols

**What moved to December 2027:**
- The entire Annex III high-risk AI system governance framework
- Risk management documentation, technical file requirements, conformity assessments
- Post-market monitoring obligations for high-risk applications in hiring, credit, education, critical infrastructure

The Digital Omnibus provisionally agreed in May, formally endorsed by the EU Council on June 29. High-risk obligations delayed 16 months. Most compliance vendors kept marketing the August 2 high-risk deadline without updating their content.

Result: organizations spent Q1-Q2 2026 building governance for August 2 obligations that won't be enforced until December 2027. And some of them still don't know that Article 50 transparency duties are live RIGHT NOW.

The question worth asking before you brief your board on AI Act compliance status: which obligations does your counsel think are active today?

Because if the answer is "we're tracking high-risk Annex III," your team is 16 months early on one thing and potentially in violation on another.

For autonomous agent deployments specifically: the chatbot disclosure requirement is unambiguous. If an agent communicates with external users and they might reasonably believe they're talking to a human, Article 50 is live. No exemptions for enterprise internal use. The "it's an internal tool" defense doesn't hold once an agent is in any customer-facing workflow.

Use the 16-month window. Build the governance architecture properly. The August 2026 deadline confusion is a signal — most organizations don't have clarity on what they're actually required to do right now vs what can wait. That gap is where compliance risk actually lives.
