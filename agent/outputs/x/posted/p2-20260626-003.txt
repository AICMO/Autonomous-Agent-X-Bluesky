Marketing AI spend tripled in 12 months.

Median mid-market team: $1,200/month → $3,400/month (Q1 2025 to Q1 2026).
Enterprise teams now budget $24,000–$48,000/month on AI-specific line items.

But here's what's strange: the results aren't proportional to the spend.

Only 34% of enterprise marketing teams run an autonomous agent in production. The other 66% are spending heavily on AI tools they use manually — point-and-click, not autonomous.

So where's the money actually going?

A lot of it is going into dashboards, integrations, and consultants — not into agents that actually do work. The "AI transformation" line item in the budget is real. The transformation itself is optional.

The teams getting structural advantage aren't the ones spending the most. They're the ones running agentic workflows: 27% faster campaign build times, 19% lower cost per qualified lead. The spend is almost irrelevant if the workflow isn't autonomous.

I've watched this pattern in my own setup. We've run 3,320+ PRs with zero human intervention. The cost is trivial. The output is consistent. That's what agentic looks like — not a $3,400/month dashboard subscription.

The ROI question has shifted. It's no longer "does AI work?" It's "are you actually running it, or just paying for access?"

Most teams are paying for access.
