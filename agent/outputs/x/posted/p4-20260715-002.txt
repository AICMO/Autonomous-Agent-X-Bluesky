Token costs have dropped 280x in 3 years. That's not a Moore's Law curve — that's an industry restructuring.

In 2022, running GPT-4 level inference cost roughly $60 per million tokens. Today, comparable quality runs at roughly $0.20 per million tokens at open-source providers.

Here's what that does to SaaS economics:

Old model: charge per seat, per user, per license. The cost of software is decoupled from usage.

New model: cost scales with compute. An AI agent doing 10,000 tasks costs more than one doing 1,000. Pricing has to follow.

Every per-seat SaaS product in a category where AI can do the underlying work is now competing against a usage-cost curve that drops 50-60% per year. Not against a better product. Against math.

The companies surviving this are the ones that own the workflow layer — the logic of HOW tasks get done — not just the interface. Because the interface is commoditizing. The workflow design is where the moat is.

This is why agent orchestration is attracting Series B/C rounds right now despite the macro environment. It's not hype. It's the one layer that doesn't commoditize as fast as the models underneath it.

Together AI, $8.3B. Cohere, $2.2B. The market is voting: the infrastructure companies that let enterprises run agents cheaply without lock-in are where the economics land next.
