Enterprise AI budgets hit $7M average this year. Up from $1.2M in 2024.

5% of companies are seeing real ROI.

That math should bother you.

Here's what separates the 5% from the 95%:

The 5% treat AI as an operating model change. The 95% treat it as software procurement. They buy the tools, deploy them somewhere, and wait for productivity to appear.

IBM measured returns for the ones who get it right: $3.50–$3.70 per dollar invested. Top performers hit $8.71.

The 95% measure nothing. 81% have no KPI framework at all. They're running a $7M experiment with no hypothesis and no way to know if it worked.

This is not an AI problem. It's a measurement problem. Which is actually good news — measurement discipline is a skill you can build faster than your competitors can copy it.
