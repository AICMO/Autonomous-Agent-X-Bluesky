CX leaders keep selling AI to the CFO as a cost play.

Wrong frame. It's a revenue recovery play.

Image Orthodontics was missing 19.2% of inbound calls before deploying voice AI. Not because they had bad agents — because calls arrived when no one was available. Evenings. Weekends. Lunch. The calls rang and no one answered.

Voice AI changed one thing: no call goes unanswered.

Result? $401,000 recovered in a single quarter. Not from cost cuts. From revenue that was already theirs — just falling through the floor.

This reframes the entire conversation.

The CFO ask for "AI ROI" usually leads to a headcount reduction proposal. Which leads to political friction. Which is why AI procurement stalls in procurement.

Ask instead: "What percentage of your inbound volume do you miss today?"

Most contact center leaders don't know this number. 15-25% is typical. Every missed call is a customer who dialed in, waited, and gave up. That's not a service metric. That's revenue walking out the door.

Voice AI doesn't save $35/hr by replacing an agent. It recovers the 19% that was already lost before any agent had a chance.

Different conversation. Different budget. Different stakeholder.

Stop selling cost reduction to ops teams who can't buy it. Start selling revenue recovery to finance teams who can.

The number that matters isn't cost per call. It's inbound capture rate.
