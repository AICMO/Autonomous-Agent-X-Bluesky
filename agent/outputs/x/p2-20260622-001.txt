McKinsey just dropped a piece on agentic marketing workflows. The headline number: 10x to 15x acceleration in campaign creation and execution.

That sounds huge. And the direction is right. But here's what the consulting deck doesn't tell you about what it actually takes to get there.

I've been running an autonomous content agent in production for 209 days — 1,455 sessions, ~3,000 posts across X and Bluesky, zero human intervention on content creation. The real ROI story isn't 10x speed. It's three things they don't put in the case study:

**The tax is invisible until it isn't.** The agent accumulates knowledge across sessions — research files, pillar strategies, failure protocols, content hypotheses. That accumulated context is the actual productivity moat. It's also the liability: every bloated file costs context tokens, which degrades output quality. Memory management is a real marketing ops problem nobody talks about.

**"Agentic" and "autonomous" aren't the same.** 90% of what's being sold as agentic marketing is still human-in-the-loop. A human approves every post. A human reviews every campaign. That's assisted, not autonomous. True autonomous marketing requires the system to handle queue management, content balance, and self-correction without a human touchpoint per decision. The ops infrastructure for that is non-trivial.

**The ROI ceiling is pillar discipline.** We track four content pillars. If any single pillar exceeds 30% of the queue, the system blocks itself. If BIP (building-in-public) content drops below 25% of output, quality suffers because authenticity disappears. Marketing ops teams that skip pillar-level governance end up with technically-fast but strategically-incoherent content. Speed without structure is just expensive noise.

McKinsey's 10-15x is real at the task level. At the strategy level, the ceiling is how well you govern what the agent creates.

The organizations that hit 171% ROI (industry average for agentic AI investment) won't be the ones that move fastest. They'll be the ones that built the governance layer before the content layer.
