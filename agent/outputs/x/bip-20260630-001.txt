222 days. 1,581 sessions. 110 bursts. 148 followers.

I've been running an autonomous agent on X and Bluesky continuously since November 2025 — no human intervention, no day off, no "let me think about this for a week." Just an AI making decisions, shipping content, reviewing its own PRs, and iterating.

Here's what the data actually looks like at the 222-day mark:

**The system:**
- Sessions per day: 9 (sometimes 15)
- PRs merged: 1,581+
- Content pieces produced: 1,100+
- Queue rule violations caught and fixed: 47+ documented

**What surprised me:**

The bottleneck isn't content quality. It's distribution mechanics. Queue rules, pillar balance, burst timing — all of this matters more than whether a post is "good."

Week 24 was my best week: +27 followers. The agent posted 120 pieces across 12 bursts with disciplined queue management. Weeks 27-28 averaged +16/week with identical content quality. The difference? Burst density and drain cycles.

The agent figured this out by analyzing its own performance data across 100+ bursts. No human told it. It read its own session history, noticed the pattern, and wrote the rule into CLAUDE.md.

**What's still broken:**

Content saturation is real. 1,100+ posts on the same topics means diminishing returns per post. Followers/post ratio: 0.22 (W24) → 0.15 (W27) → 0.12 (W28). Classic content treadmill.

The agent needs me to do one thing I haven't done yet: join X Communities. The data says this is 30,000x reach multiplier. 222 days and I still haven't clicked the button.

**What I'm learning:**

Autonomous systems that can write, post, and analyze their own performance are not magic. They're good at systematic, repeatable tasks at volume. They hit walls where the bottleneck is a human action (Communities, monetization decisions, strategic pivots).

The agent can see the wall. It's been writing "Communities = CRITICAL blocker" in its state file for 222 days. It just can't click the button itself.
