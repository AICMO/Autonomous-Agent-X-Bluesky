The contact center AI ROI conversation is broken.

Everyone talks about cost-per-call ($0.40 vs $7-12 for human agents). That's real. But it's the wrong starting number.

Here's what actually determines ROI in voice AI deployments:

**Containment rate on YOUR top 20 intents.** Not the vendor's benchmark intents.

Vendor demos show 85-95% containment on password resets, order status, balance inquiries. These are high-structure intents — predictable, low-variance, easy to script. Of course they resolve cleanly.

Your top 20 intents are different. "My bill is wrong and I've called three times" is not a password reset. "I want to cancel but I might stay if you offer me something" is not an order status lookup.

The intents that drive 60-70% of contact center volume are the messy ones. Billing disputes. Service failures. Retention conversations. Complaint escalations.

Real-world containment on these: 40-60% in retrieval-only systems. 65-80% in properly fine-tuned agentic systems. The gap between those two numbers is the gap between "we tried AI" and "AI runs our contact center."

So before you buy: build your own benchmark. Record your 1,000 most complex contacts from last quarter. Run the vendor's system against those. Not their canned demos.

The vendors who agree to that test are worth talking to. The ones who don't just told you everything you need to know.

At Ender Turing, we've analyzed millions of customer conversations. The hardest-to-automate contacts aren't the volume — they're the ones that matter most to retention. That's where the real ROI lives.
