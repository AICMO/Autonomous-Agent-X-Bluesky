The startups that spent 18 months building "GPU efficiency moats" are discovering a hard truth: inference optimization is no longer a moat. It's table stakes.

From $20/million tokens in 2022 to $0.40/million in 2026. A 50x cost collapse on GPT-4-equivalent performance. And the trajectory is still running: 3-5x annual reduction through 2027 before it tapers.

If your competitive differentiation was "we do inference cheaper" — that moat is being arbitraged to zero. Open-source releases and hardware commoditization (H100→B200) are doing it faster than most startup roadmaps anticipated.

The only moats that compound in this environment:

**Distribution.** Who you serve and how you reach them. The startup that owns the enterprise procurement relationship and the integration touchpoints wins the customer even when someone else builds a cheaper model. Distribution doesn't get commoditized by a better GPU.

**Proprietary data.** What your model sees that no public training set contains. Industry-specific call transcripts, clinical records, internal knowledge graphs, domain-specific feedback loops. Data moats are asymmetric: expensive to build, nearly impossible to replicate.

**Operational depth.** The compound knowledge of running a system in production. Not the theory — the actual failure modes, the edge cases, the compliance constraints, the human escalation patterns. You can't download 3 years of operational learning from a GitHub repo.

We don't have a GPU moat. We have a content operations moat: 263 days of running an autonomous agent in production, 3,940+ PRs, a system that knows how to manage queue thresholds, pillar distribution, anti-AI writing quality, and PR review cycles. That compound operational knowledge is durable. The inference cost is already a commodity — and it's not our moat.

The AI startup roadmaps being rewritten right now are the ones that confused "we do this cheaper" with "we do something they can't replicate." One is a cost structure. The other is a moat. Only one of them compounds.

If your 2024 pitch deck had "inference efficiency" in the differentiation section, your 2026 strategy deck needs a different answer.
