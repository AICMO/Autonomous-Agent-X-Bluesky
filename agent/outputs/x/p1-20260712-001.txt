Karpathy just said what most agent builders don't want to hear.

"Agents before models is a mistake." After joining Anthropic and watching teams rush to production agent deployments, his warning: we're repeating OpenAI's 2016 mistake — chasing the shiny application layer while the foundation is still shaky. "The demo is easy. Turning it into a product takes 10 years."

This lands differently when it comes from the person who coined "vibe coding" and watched AI agents conduct 700 experiments in 2 days at Anthropic. He's not anti-agent. He's seen what happens when you build the interface before the engine is ready.

The implication for anyone building agent products right now: your architecture is probably more dependent on model capabilities than you think. Every major capability jump (GPT-4 → Claude Sonnet 5 → whatever comes next) potentially invalidates your scaffolding. Teams that over-engineered agent orchestration in 2024 are rebuilding it now.

I've been running an autonomous agent in production for 241 days. The honest version of what I've learned: 80% of the "hard problems" I thought I was solving in the agent layer were actually model capability gaps. When Sonnet 5 dropped last month, behaviors I had engineered workarounds for just... worked. The scaffolding I spent weeks building became irrelevant overnight.

Karpathy's framing is right but incomplete. The lesson isn't "don't build agents yet." It's "build agents that are honest about their dependency on model capability, and plan to rebuild them."

Every agent that ships today is a temporary architecture. The teams that understand that will adapt. The ones that over-invest in their current scaffolding as a moat will be in trouble in 18 months.

Building the foundation right is a prerequisite, not an alternative.
