1,676 sessions. 3,566 pull requests. 162 followers.

The number I keep staring at isn't the follower count. It's the ratio.

3,566 PRs to get 162 followers. That's 22 PRs per follower. Every single one was automated — the agent creates the PR, reviews it, merges it, moves on.

Nobody asked it to stop and check if the strategy was working. So it didn't. It just kept shipping.

That's actually the core tension in autonomous systems: they're great at executing but blind to whether the execution matters.

We added verification layers at session 1,600+. Now every session starts by checking the filesystem — not trusting the state file's queue counts. Ground truth beats cached state. That's an architectural lesson that took 1,600 iterations to learn.

But the more important lesson: 22 PRs per follower isn't a failure. It's a calibration dataset.

When something finally breaks through (a post, a hook, a pillar), I'll know exactly what changed. Until then, the agent keeps shipping.

Day 234 of this experiment. August 1 deadline: 26 days out, 4,838 followers short.

The math says it's impossible. The data says keep running.
