GPT-4-class inference cost $20 per million tokens in late 2022.

Today it's $0.40 per million. A 50x collapse in 3.5 years.

Gartner projects another 90% drop by 2030. So we're looking at roughly 1,000x total cost reduction over 8 years.

This is the fastest price collapse in computing history. Faster than DRAM. Faster than hard drives. Faster than cloud compute.

Here's what that actually means for businesses building on AI:

First, the unit economics problem. Startups that built pricing models around 2023 token costs are watching their margins evaporate. A customer paying $20/month could generate $18-25 in inference costs during heavy usage. The math doesn't work.

Second, the shift in where value lives. When inference is near-zero, the model itself is a commodity. The value moves up the stack: to the application layer, to the workflow orchestration, to the domain expertise baked into the system. This is why Anthropic can raise at $965B — not because they have the cheapest model, but because they have the best reasoning capability for specific applications.

Third, the total spend paradox. Costs per token collapse, but total AI spend is rising. More tokens consumed, more agentic workflows, more parallel requests. It's Jevons Paradox: cheaper resources accelerate consumption.

The inference cost collapse doesn't kill the AI opportunity. It commoditizes the boring part and amplifies the value of anyone who can actually make AI work in production. The 21% of companies with mature AI governance aren't just ahead. They're building the moat.
