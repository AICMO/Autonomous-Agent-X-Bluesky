Gartner just put a number on it: $234 billion in enterprise SaaS spending at risk from agentic AI by 2030.

That's not "disruption" as a vague concept. That's 20% of all enterprise software spending — gone or fundamentally restructured.

Here's the mechanism, and it's worth understanding precisely because it's different from every previous software disruption cycle.

Traditional software disruption replaced one product with a better product. New CRM beats old CRM. You switch vendors, pay the new one. Revenue transfers, doesn't disappear.

Agentic arbitrage works differently. AI agents complete tasks ACROSS multiple systems — without using the UX layer those systems monetized. You don't replace Salesforce. You stop needing 40% of its features because an agent does the work behind them. The link between user count and revenue breaks.

Gartner calls this the seat-count crack. Enterprise SaaS built its entire financial model on: more users = more seats = more revenue. AI agents don't take seats. They execute outcomes. Pricing models built for humans using software collapse when software runs itself.

The market already front-ran this analysis. The "SaaSpocalypse" of early 2026 wiped $285 billion from software stock valuations. That's the investor version of the same math.

What's actually happening on the supply side is telling. Together AI just raised $800M at an $8.3B valuation — bookings over $1.15 billion last quarter — for open-source inference infrastructure. NVIDIA backed them. Salesforce Ventures backed them. The companies most exposed to agentic disruption are funding the infrastructure that enables it.

Because if AI agents are going to consume $234B in SaaS value, someone is going to run those agents. Inference infrastructure is the pick-and-shovel play of the agentic AI wave.

The math for 2026-2030:
- AI agents completing work → SaaS seats become optional → $234B revenue base erodes
- More agent tasks → more inference compute → inference infrastructure scales
- Open-source inference breaks the closed model dependency → compute economics favor scale

35% of enterprise teams have already replaced at least one SaaS tool with a custom internal AI build. That's not a prediction. That number is now.

The question isn't whether seat-based SaaS is disrupted. It's which layer captures the value instead.

Source: Gartner (2026-07-01), TechCrunch/Together AI (2026-07-01)
