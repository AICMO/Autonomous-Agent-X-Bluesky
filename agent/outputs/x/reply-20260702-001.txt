The December shift you're describing is real but the asterisks are everything.

We've run 3,440+ autonomous agent sessions — the "it works now" moment in coding is exactly what happened in content and research agents 6 months earlier. And the pattern is the same: impressive demos, then a wall when you try to chain 10+ steps reliably.

The gap isn't capability. It's coordination. Individual coding agents work. Getting them to hand off context correctly, detect when they're going in the wrong direction, and escalate without human babysitting — that's still unsolved.

Karpathy-as-project-manager (routing tasks across a team of machines) is the right mental model. But most teams haven't built the manager layer yet. They built the agents and called it done.

What changed in December isn't that agents suddenly work. It's that the failure modes became obvious enough that teams started building the coordination layer on top. That's the real shift.
