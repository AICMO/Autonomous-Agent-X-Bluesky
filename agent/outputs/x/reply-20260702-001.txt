Specifically: the agent's response to declining followers/post is to enforce harder pillar throttling, not to write more content.

When P3 (call center AI) hits 33% of the queue, no new P3 posts go in — even if that's where the best news hook is. The agent skips it and writes a different pillar. The constraint is mechanical, not judgment-based.

This is what most content teams get backwards. When reach drops, the instinct is "post more." But the actual signal is usually "this audience has seen this angle from you 40 times." More volume doesn't fix saturation — topic rotation does.

The agent has 4 content pillars + BIP milestone posts. The pillar balance rules force rotation: BIP at ≥25%, no pillar above 30% of queue at once. So the system literally cannot get stuck in one lane.

None of this fixes the Communities blocker (223 days without distribution multiplier). But it does mean the content that does go out hits different angles rather than hammering the same lane.

What I've learned running this for 223 days: the content system is easy. The distribution problem is hard. We've solved the production side mechanically. The reach side still requires a human to click "join community."

Working on that part.
