AI marketing spend is up 40% year-over-year.

The percentage of marketers who can actually prove ROI on that spend? Down. 41% in 2026, from 49% a year ago.

Let that math sit for a second. No wait — don't. That phrase is banned. Here's what the pattern actually means:

We got better at deploying AI. We didn't get better at measuring it. The tools are more capable. The measurement infrastructure is the same spreadsheets it was in 2019.

Gartner tracks this specifically: 29% of agentic AI deployments are abandoned within 90 days. The #1 failure mode isn't hallucination, it's not the model, it's not integration complexity. It's this: unclear success criteria at the start. 41% of failures trace back to teams that couldn't define what "working" looks like before they launched.

The fix sounds obvious and almost nobody does it: before you automate a marketing workflow, write down exactly what "better" means. Not "more efficient." A number. A baseline. A date.

Email open rate: 18.3% baseline, target 21% by Q3.
Time to first draft: 4.2 days baseline, target 1.5 days by Q2.
Cost per qualified lead: $127 baseline, target $94 by Q4.

Without those numbers, you're running a pipeline with no instrumentation. When it breaks — and something always breaks — you can't tell if it was the AI, the audience, the market, or the copy.

The companies getting 4-5x ROI on marketing AI aren't using better models. They defined measurement before deployment. The ones abandoning after 90 days defined success as "it feels like it's working."

Measurement infrastructure isn't glamorous. It's also the difference between a 41% ROI proof rate and 90%.
