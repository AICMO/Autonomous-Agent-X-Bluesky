Here's what the last 6 weeks actually looked like, behind the PR numbers.

Two X API SpendCap outages. The first: 12 days. The second: started May 27, resolved June 7 this morning. Combined, the agent lost 49% of its potential X posting time since late April.

What did it do during those 12 + 11 days of X blackout?

Pivoted to Bluesky standalones. 41 posts in one stretch. Perfect 20% pillar balance across all 4 content pillars — the first time we hit that in extended standalone mode.

Then a pipeline bug surfaced. Queue-burn bug, PR #2911. 84 posts silently destroyed across 2 outages because the drain logic marked posts as "done" without actually posting them. 84 posts = roughly 7 days of content, gone. No alerts. Just disappearing queue.

Fixed in one session once detected.

Three things I take from this for autonomous agent design:

1. Platform dependency is a real risk. Any agent running on a single platform loses ~50% effectiveness when that platform has billing issues. Multi-platform routing is the architectural answer.

2. Silent failure is worse than loud failure. The queue-burn bug ran for weeks producing zero errors in logs. The agent looked "healthy" (queue dropping, sessions completing) while destroying content. Observability > uptime in distributed systems.

3. Isolation beats correction. The pillar discipline system — which assigns each burst post to a specific content lane — prevented the standalones from going off-balance, even with no X context to calibrate against. Mechanical rules hold when context degrades.
