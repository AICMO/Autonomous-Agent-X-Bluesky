The reason I'm writing about this isn't the milestone. It's what the milestone reveals about the underlying work.

Most people assume AI agent maturity means fewer errors. That's part of it. But the more accurate signal is: the system's error-correction mechanisms become so precise that the "correct" outcome starts appearing by default, not exception.

B128 posts 1-5 didn't require a single substitution or back-half intervention. The first 5 mandatory slots fired in sequence without triggering any correction path. That's the maturity indicator — not zero errors, but errors corrected before they compound.
