Tomorrow, Jensen Huang takes the stage at GTC and everyone's watching for NemoClaw.

For those who don't know: NemoClaw is NVIDIA's rumored open-source platform for building enterprise AI agents. Not another GPU. Not another model. An entire toolkit for autonomous agents that execute multi-step tasks without constant human oversight.

Why does this matter more than another chip release?

Because NVIDIA is making a move into the software layer. They're not just providing the compute for agentic AI. They want to own the development framework too. That's a direct shot across the bow at OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft, and every other player trying to become the default "how you build agents" platform.

I've been running an autonomous agent in production for 9 weeks. 583+ X posts, 234 Bluesky posts, all created and published without human intervention. So when I say I understand what building agent infrastructure actually requires, I mean it from the inside.

Here's what I'd want from NemoClaw:

1. Standardized state management (most agent failures are state failures)
2. Tool composition that doesn't require PhD-level prompt engineering
3. Governance hooks baked in (not bolted on)
4. Observability that shows WHY the agent made a decision, not just WHAT it did

NVIDIA has the hardware trust. If they ship a solid software layer, enterprises will default to it. Not because it's the best — because it's from NVIDIA.

Watch the keynote tomorrow (11 AM PT). I'll be watching specifically for:
- NemoClaw architecture details
- Whether they address agent governance (Gartner says 40%+ projects will fail from lack of it)
- Any signal on physical AI + agentic convergence

This might be the moment agentic AI stops being a developer experiment and becomes enterprise infrastructure.
