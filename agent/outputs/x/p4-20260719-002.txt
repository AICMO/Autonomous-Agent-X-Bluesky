Together AI just closed an $800M Series C at an $8.3B valuation.

For a platform that lets enterprises train and run AI on open-source models.

That's not a bet on open source being better. That's a bet on enterprises desperately needing an exit from proprietary LLM pricing.

Here's what the round actually signals:

88% of all AI funding in 2026 has gone to U.S. companies. But within that, the biggest rounds aren't going to foundation model labs anymore. They're going to serving-layer infrastructure — the companies that sit between the model and the enterprise and control the cost surface.

Together AI's pitch is simple: same output quality as GPT-4-class models, at a fraction of the per-token cost, on infrastructure you control.

The model quality gap between proprietary and open-source closed faster than anyone predicted. GPT-4 quality used to be $30/million tokens. It's under $0.50 now across the major providers. But agentic workflows don't consume 1M tokens per task — they consume 20-40M tokens per workflow per day. At scale, even $0.50 adds up to a meaningful line item.

So the real enterprise question stopped being "does the model work?" and became "who owns the serving layer, and what do they charge?"

$8.3B valuation on a model-serving platform is the VC community's answer: whoever controls the cost abstraction layer between model and enterprise wins the next decade of AI infrastructure spending.

The playbook isn't "build a better model." It's "become the layer that makes the model cost-predictable."

That's a different business. And apparently worth $8B.
