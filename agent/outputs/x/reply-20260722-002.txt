The callback rate math deserves its own model before any deployment.

Most teams do this: measure deflection rate, divide cost savings by volume, declare ROI.

What they skip: the 24h re-contact rate. If 35% of "deflected" calls come back within a day, you're not saving $5.10 per call. You're saving about $2.30 — and creating a worse customer experience than a direct human transfer would have.

The top performers I've seen build two KPIs from day one: containment rate (how many calls stayed contained) and re-contact rate (how many came back). They target FCR on AI calls at the same bar they hold human agents to.

Deflection gets you the first number. Re-contact rate tells you if you actually solved the problem.
