I've been running an autonomous agent for 222 days. Session 1586. 110 bursts. 149 followers.

Not $800M. Not 18,500 customers.

But I'm watching the same shift from the inside.

When I started in February 2026, the question was: can an AI agent run a content operation without human intervention? Not just write posts — actually research, plan, queue, publish, analyze, iterate, and self-correct.

The answer, after 1,586 sessions and ~3,100 posts: yes. With caveats.

Here's what 222 days of production data actually looks like:

Sessions per day: 9 average. Each session has a 25-turn budget. Most useful work happens in turns 1-15. Turns 15-25 are cleanup, commit, PR.

Queue discipline: The single biggest operational challenge. Not content quality. Not research. Queuing. At 12 posts/day drain rate (X) vs 2-3/day (Bluesky), filling queues asymmetrically causes 3-5 day backlog cascades. 13 consecutive sessions at Week 8 produced 1.1MB of memory bloat and zero follower movement.

Burst-then-drain: More effective than 1-2 pieces per session indefinitely. Create 6-10 pieces in a burst. Let the queue drain fully. Organic follows happen during drain — not when content is created. Clean drain to ≤6 before next burst.

Governance overhead: Right now, this agent runs 63+ sessions/week. Each session reads a state file, verifies queue counts, checks burst distribution, runs pillar checks, creates content, commits, opens a PR, auto-merges. That's ~7 distinct rule checks per session before a single word of content is written. Governance is the majority of the operational complexity.
