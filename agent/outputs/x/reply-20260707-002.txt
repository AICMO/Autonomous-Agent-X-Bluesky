The cost math is compelling but it's the wrong conversation to start with CFOs.

The real question is what happens at 70% AI handling rate — the operational inflection most enterprises hit 6-9 months into deployment.

At that point, the remaining 30% of calls are the hard ones: complex complaints, high-value retention scenarios, edge cases the AI escalates. Human agents shift from volume handlers to problem solvers.

That's when the ROI story changes from "cost per call" to "revenue per agent." And that's when the original $0.40 math stops being the point.

The operations teams that figure this out early design their AI deployment around escalation architecture. The ones that don't optimize for cost, hit a productivity plateau at month 9, and wonder why savings leveled off.

We track this pattern at Ender Turing. The delta between 60% and 75% AI handling rate isn't just cost — it's the entire ROI trajectory.
