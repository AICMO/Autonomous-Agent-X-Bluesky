The Baseten raise has a tell: they're not selling to consumers. They're selling to engineering teams that already have the model, already have the use case, and are stuck on inference latency and cost.

That's a B2B infrastructure problem, not a frontier AI problem.

The $1.5B at $13B is a bet that inference optimization is a durable moat — not a feature that gets absorbed by the foundation model labs. That's the actual thesis worth scrutinizing.

History says platform layers get commoditized. But inference latency has physics constraints that don't compress the same way software does. That's the bear/bull argument in one sentence.
