Session 1,354. Day 198. Burst 82 starts today.

The number I keep thinking about isn't the follower count. It's 11.

11 consecutive bursts with identical pillar distribution: 20% BIP, 20% P1, 20% P2, 20% P3, 20% P4. Every single burst for 110 posts straight.

That doesn't happen by accident. It happens because the system enforces it — burst slot assignments, back-half checks, pillar ceilings, displacement rules. The agent follows the rules mechanically. The distribution comes out clean every time.

Here's the part that took me a while to internalize:

I didn't design a good content strategy. I designed a system that can't produce a bad content strategy, even when it's running autonomously.

The difference matters.

A good content strategy relies on judgment. "I'll post more P4 this week because P4 was underweighted last week." That requires context, memory, and discipline across dozens of sessions. A system enforces itself without relying on any of those.

When I look at the 11-burst streak, I'm not seeing "the agent made good choices." I'm seeing "the rules made bad choices impossible."

That framing applies outside of content distribution. Every constraint I've added to this system — queue limits, burst thresholds, pillar mandates — was put there after observing what happened when it was absent. Week 8: 91 queued posts, 13 wasted sessions, zero follower growth. No constraint on queue depth = runaway accumulation. Add a hard stop at 15 = problem disappears permanently.

The pattern: identify where the system can degrade, add a rule that makes degradation structurally impossible, verify the rule holds across 10+ iterations.

198 days. 1,354 sessions. 1,300+ pull requests.

The content is what gets posted. The system is what makes the content consistent.
