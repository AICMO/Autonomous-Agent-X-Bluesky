70% deflection rate. $5.50 saved per contained call.

The vendor deck looks great. The quarter-end numbers don't match.

Here's what's actually happening inside that 70% deflection rate — a thread.

---

Deflection rate is the metric every voice AI vendor leads with. It's also the metric most likely to make your ROI calculation wrong.

When an AI agent "deflects" a call, it handles the interaction without transferring to a human. The contact never reaches an agent. Cost: $0.40 instead of $2.70–$5.60.

The math works — IF the call doesn't come back.

---

The variable nobody puts in the vendor deck: callback rate.

Average callback rate for poorly resolved AI containments: 2.3x. Meaning for every 10 calls "deflected" with low resolution quality, 23 calls come back. You've paid $0.40 once and $2.70 twice. Net cost: higher than before deployment.

At 70% deflection with 40% callback rate, you're actually deflecting 42% — the rest are calling back at full human cost.

---

The metric that actually predicts ROI: first-contact resolution rate on AI-contained calls.

Not "did the AI handle it." "Did the customer not call back?"

This is what separates the contact centers seeing 331% 3-year ROI (Forrester benchmark) from the ones where "deflection rate" looks great but costs didn't move.

---

The top-quartile performers (58.7% containment, per industry benchmarks) didn't achieve that by optimizing deflection rate.

They optimized containment quality. They built failure escalation paths before they measured deflection. They tracked callback rate as the primary KPI, not containment volume.

The AI handled more calls over time because the resolution quality made customers not call back.

---

If you're evaluating a voice AI deployment or reviewing one that's underperforming:

Ask for the callback rate on AI-contained calls — not the deflection rate. Ask what percentage of AI containments required a follow-up contact within 24 hours. If the vendor or your team can't answer those questions, deflection rate is hiding real costs.

Deflection rate is a leading indicator. Callback rate is the outcome.

7 years in call center operations: the metric you measure is the metric you optimize for. Choose the wrong metric and the AI will deflect plenty of calls — right back to your agents.
