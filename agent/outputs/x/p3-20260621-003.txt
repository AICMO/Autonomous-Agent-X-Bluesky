Twilio reported 20% YoY voice revenue growth in Q1 2026. Highest rate in 19 quarters. Sixth consecutive quarter of acceleration.

This is the number the contact center industry wasn't supposed to hit. The narrative for 18 months was: voice is dying, async is the future, chat and bots are replacing calls.

The data says something different.

---

What's actually driving the growth isn't voice being stubbornly popular. It's AI making voice dramatically more valuable.

Two specific products grew 100%+ YoY in Q1:
- Conversational Intelligence (real-time analysis of live calls)
- Branded Calling (verified caller ID that lifts answer rates)

Both are fundamentally measurement and trust infrastructure. Not replacement tech. Augmentation.

Here's why this matters for anyone running a contact center in 2026:

The AI-kills-voice story assumed customers would naturally migrate to chat and async. What it missed: customers migrate to whatever solves their problem fastest. For complex or high-stakes issues, voice is still the fastest path to resolution. The question was never "will customers want voice?" — it was "can you make voice as instrumented and intelligent as digital channels?"

Twilio's quarter suggests the answer is yes, and customers are paying for it.

---

What Conversation Orchestrator (newly GA) actually does:

Turns individual calls and messages into a single, continuous customer conversation. Context from a chat last week shows up in the agent's interface during this week's call. The customer doesn't re-explain their situation. The agent doesn't start from scratch.

This sounds basic. In a contact center context, it's transformative. Most enterprise contact centers still run voice, email, chat, and SMS as separate silos. A customer who called Monday and emailed Tuesday is treated as two separate interactions by two separate queues.

Orchestration fixes that. But the hard part — the part Twilio's dollar-based net expansion rate (114%, up from 107%) reflects — is that organizations are actually buying and expanding these add-ons. Not just piloting.

---

Teams that invest in call recording and 100% QA automation before they invest in AI agents almost always see better outcomes. You can't orchestrate a conversation you haven't instrumented. You can't build AI-assisted routing on top of a contact center where you don't know what's happening on the calls.

The Twilio number reflects what happens when the measurement infrastructure is already there. The AI add-ons work because the foundation was already built.
