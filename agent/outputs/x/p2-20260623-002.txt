The CMO hiring playbook is breaking.

Not because CMOs are less valuable. Because the unit economics of marketing are shifting faster than job descriptions can follow.

Data point: enterprise marketing teams added headcount through 2023 on the assumption that content velocity = headcount. More writers, more posts, more campaigns. The logic held for years.

Then agentic tools started compressing the ratio.

Here's what the current transition actually looks like at companies doing it right:

1 senior strategist now sets direction for systems that execute 40+ content pieces/week. The leverage ratio used to be 1:5 (one strategist, five writers). It's becoming 1:200+ for commodity content types.

This isn't hypothetical. BCG's data shows 240x efficiency gains in specific marketing workflows. Not all workflows — the creative, strategic, and relationship-building work still requires humans. But the production layer?

The production layer is being automated.

The strategic implication: marketing teams that don't adapt will either shrink or be restructured around different skills. The people winning aren't the ones who fight the automation — they're the ones who position themselves as the strategists directing it.

Two kinds of marketing leaders emerging right now:
- Those who understand how agentic systems work and can direct them
- Those who don't, and are competing with them for the same outcomes

The skills gap is real. The timeline is shorter than most people think.
