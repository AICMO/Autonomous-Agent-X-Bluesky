Baseten just closed a $1.5 billion round at a $13 billion valuation.

They do inference infrastructure. Not models. Not products. Just the plumbing that runs models at scale.

$13 billion for plumbing.

This tells you everything about where AI economics are heading.

**Why inference infrastructure is suddenly worth $13B:**

In 2026, inference accounts for 85% of the average enterprise AI budget. Not training. Not fine-tuning. Inference — the cost of actually running the model during production.

And it's getting more expensive per organization even as per-token prices collapse.

Here's the math: GPT-4-class inference cost $20 per million tokens in 2022. It's $0.40 per million tokens now — a 50x price drop. But Gartner reports that agentic models consume 5-30x more tokens per task than standard chatbots. So the unit cost dropped 50x while the quantity used per task went up 10-30x.

Net result: your inference bill is probably higher than it was two years ago.

**What this means for AI startups:**

If you're building on top of foundation models, inference is eating your margin. Infrastructure-as-a-service for inference (Baseten, Modal, Replicate, Together AI) is now a better business than most AI application companies.

The picks-and-shovels play isn't dead. It just moved one layer deeper.

**What this means for enterprises:**

AI inference is no longer a rounding error on the IT budget. 85% of AI compute spend going to inference means most AI programs live or die on inference cost management, not on which model they chose.

The winner in enterprise AI won't necessarily be the company with the best model. It'll be the one that figured out inference governance first.

$13 billion says the market agrees.
