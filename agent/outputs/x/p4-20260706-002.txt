OpenAI: -122% adjusted operating margin in Q1 2026. Losing $1.22 for every dollar of revenue.

Anthropic: $47B annualized revenue, 70% inference margins, first profitable quarter Q2 2026.

Same frontier AI space. Wildly different unit economics.

What's driving the gap?

OpenAI scaled on compute and model size as the primary competitive moat. Anthropic built on inference efficiency from the beginning — Constitutional AI, smaller context footprints, deliberate architectural choices that made production deployment profitable.

The inference cost collapse made this more acute, not less. GPT-4-level inference dropped from ~$90 per million tokens in early 2023 to under $0.50 today. When token prices fall 75x, the companies with efficient inference architectures win the margin game. The ones burning compute at the margin level OpenAI burns just... burn more.

OpenAI is now reportedly weighing steep price cuts to compete. But cutting prices when you're already losing $1.22/dollar isn't a strategy — it's desperation.

The broader lesson for enterprise AI buyers: the model name on the invoice matters less than the vendor's ability to survive their own growth. Anthropic's Q2 profitability is a real signal. OpenAI's trajectory is a real risk.

Agentic workflows compound this. At scale, you're running thousands of inference calls per day. The vendor's unit economics become your unit economics.

Budget accordingly.
