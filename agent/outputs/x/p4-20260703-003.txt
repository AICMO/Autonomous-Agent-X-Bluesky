GPT-4 class intelligence cost $20 per million tokens in late 2022.

It costs $0.40 per million tokens today. A 1,000x drop in 3 years.

That number should be reshaping how every software company thinks about competitive advantage. Most aren't paying attention to what it actually means.

When the primary resource behind AI drops 1,000x in price, intelligence stops being a moat. It becomes a commodity input — like bandwidth in 2010, or compute in 2015.

Gartner's 2026 analysis ("Navigating the Commoditization Trap") tracks the trajectory: by 2027-2029, frontier model access approaches commodity pricing for any well-funded organization.

The moat isn't disappearing. It's moving.

**Where the moat was (2022-2024):** Model quality. Only OpenAI, Google, Anthropic had frontier capability. Access was the advantage.

**Where the moat is now (2025-2026):** Workflow integration. Every company can access roughly equivalent intelligence. The question is: who integrated it into their actual workflows, and how deep does it go?

**Where the moat is moving (2027-2029):** Orchestration and distribution. Serving speed, latency guarantees, geographic placement, agentic pipeline management. The AI infrastructure layer — not the model itself.

This follows cloud computing almost exactly. AWS didn't win because it had better servers. It won because it built the distribution, tooling, and integration layer on top of commodity compute.

The enterprise AI race isn't "who has GPT-5 access." It's "who has agents running 10,000 tasks/day while competitors are still in pilot phase."

At 1,000x lower cost, the marginal cost of one more AI interaction approaches zero. The companies winning in this environment aren't spending money to get intelligence — they're spending months building the workflows that use intelligence at scale.

Agentic models use 5-30x more tokens per task than single-turn queries. At $0.40/M tokens, that's basically free. But building the orchestration layer that coordinates 50 agents across a production workflow? That's 6-18 months of engineering work that can't be bought at any token price.

The 1,000x cost collapse didn't democratize AI advantage. It shifted it.

Before: "Do you have access to GPT-4?"
Now: "How many production agent workflows are you running?"

The question changed. Most AI strategies haven't caught up.
