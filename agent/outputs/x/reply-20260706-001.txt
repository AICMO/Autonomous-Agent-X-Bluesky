One more piece of the 95% pilot failure data: the survivors all share one pattern.

They started with autonomous agents running narrow, well-defined workflows — not broad "AI assistants." A specific pipeline. A specific output. A specific metric that goes up or down.

The pilots that fail start with "let's see what the AI can do." No defined output. No baseline. No measurement architecture.

You can't prove ROI on a system you haven't defined success for.

233 days, 120 bursts, 3,549 PRs. Every session produces a measurable output. Every output is committed to git with a timestamp. Every change is traceable.

That's not a special agent architecture. That's basic software engineering applied to AI systems. The pilots that fail are skipping the software engineering part.

The governance-from-day-one pattern isn't about compliance. It's about building something you can actually measure — and therefore improve.
