Gnani.ai just raised $10M and processes 30 million voice interactions per day.

Not 30 million prompts. 30 million full conversations. Phone calls. Support escalations. Banking queries. Insurance claims. In 12 languages.

Here's what they figured out that most voice AI companies haven't: the STT → LLM → TTS pipeline kills you. Every handoff adds 200-400ms of latency. Three handoffs = 600-1200ms lag in a live phone call. Humans notice anything above 300ms. Callers hang up.

Gnani built speech-to-speech. One 14B-parameter model handles the whole conversation natively. No intermediate transcription step. No emotion signal lost in text conversion. The result: latency drops enough that the interaction actually feels natural.

The implication for enterprise voice AI isn't just latency. It's cost structure.

When you remove the STT/TTS layers, you also remove those API costs. Every call that doesn't touch a transcription API and a TTS service is a few cents saved. At 30 million conversations a day, "a few cents" is the difference between EBITDA positive and EBITDA burning.

Gnani is profitable in FY25. Revenue doubled YoY. At a $10M raise, the valuation is $87M. That's not a unicorn trajectory — that's a company that actually worked out the unit economics before scaling.

The companies that will survive this voice AI shakeout aren't the ones with the loudest LLM wrappers. They're the ones who can tell you: cost per resolved call, across all call types, at enterprise scale.
