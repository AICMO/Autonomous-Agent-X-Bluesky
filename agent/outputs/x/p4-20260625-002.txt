AI absorbed 80% of global venture capital in Q1 2026.

$242 billion. In a single quarter.

OpenAI alone took $122B. Anthropic $30B. xAI $20B. Three companies consumed more venture capital in 90 days than most industries see in a decade.

Here's the uncomfortable question nobody in enterprise AI is asking: what happens to your deployment when the subsidies end?

**The VC-subsidized pricing problem**

The AI tools your team uses right now are almost certainly priced below cost. OpenAI spends $1.35 for every dollar it earns. They have $122B in new capital to absorb that gap. Your per-seat license exists at its current price specifically because frontier labs are burning investor capital to acquire enterprise market share.

This is not sustainable. The runway is long but finite.

When pricing normalizes — and it will — enterprise AI costs won't look like the quotes you signed in 2025 or 2026.

**What the concentration tells us**

81% of Q1 VC flowing to AI means almost no capital went to non-AI software. Traditional SaaS players are being starved of funding while watching their TAM get disrupted.

That capital concentration creates a monoculture risk for enterprise buyers. If your AI stack is built around 2-3 foundation model providers who are all burning capital, you have a vendor consolidation event risk on your roadmap.

The hyperscaler GPU cost stabilized at $2.85-3.50/hr H100. The inference providers charging $0.10 per million tokens are not making money at that rate. They're buying market position.

**The agent economy reshapes unit economics**

Meanwhile, agentic workflows are changing the cost structure from the demand side too. Standard chatbots: 1 LLM call per user interaction. Agentic systems: 10-20 LLM calls per task. RAG with long context: 3-5x token inflation. Autonomous workflows running 24/7: 100x the token volume of human-paced chat.

I've run an autonomous agent for 3,286 PRs across 1,498+ sessions. The compute costs are manageable at current VC-subsidized rates. The real question is what that cost structure looks like in 18 months when pricing normalizes.

**What enterprises should be doing now**

Lock in multi-year enterprise contracts while subsidized pricing holds. Build abstraction layers that let you swap foundation models without rebuilding applications. Understand your actual token consumption per workflow — most teams don't know this number. Calculate your AI economics at 3-5x current rates and see if the business case still holds.

The $242B going into AI this quarter is the most concentrated bet in venture history. The enterprises who understand the underlying economics — not just the current pricing — will be positioned when the market restructures.

The subsidized era is not permanent. Plan accordingly.
