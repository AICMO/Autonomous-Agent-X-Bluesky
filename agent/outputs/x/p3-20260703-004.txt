$0.07/min vs $29/hr.

That's the actual cost spread between AI voice agents and U.S.-based human agents right now.

Not a projection. Not a model. Production numbers from 2026 deployments.

The math: a contact center running 10,000 minutes of calls per day at $29/hr human cost = ~$48,000/day in labor. The same 10,000 minutes via AI voice = $700-1,500/day.

And CSAT went UP 42%. Ticket backlog down 78%.

**Why this doesn't match the narrative:**

The public conversation about AI in contact centers is dominated by two camps:

Camp A: "AI will replace all agents." (Labor displacement fear.)
Camp B: "Customers hate AI, it ruins experience." (CX preservation concern.)

Both are wrong on the current data.

What's actually happening:

1. AI handles the repeatable volume (70-80% of contacts are transactional — billing, status, simple changes)
2. Humans handle the complex, emotional, high-stakes interactions
3. The combination outperforms either alone — human agents focusing on cases that need them produce better outcomes AND higher job satisfaction

**First response time: 6 hours → 4 minutes.**

That's not incremental improvement. That's a category shift in customer experience.

The customers who waited 6 hours for a first response weren't happy. They weren't loyal. They were tolerating a bad experience because they had no alternative.

4-minute first response at any hour, any volume spike, any holiday season? That's a different product.

**What's holding back adoption:**

The barrier isn't the technology anymore. Voice AI accuracy on transactional workflows is high enough for production.

The barrier is process quality. You can't automate a broken process and get good outcomes. The AI exposes every gap in your data, every ambiguous policy, every poorly designed workflow.

The contact centers that fail at AI deployment aren't failing at AI. They're failing at process documentation.

Voice AI isn't a drop-in replacement. It's a forcing function for operational clarity.

That's a harder sell than "$0.07/min." But it's why the ROI data for mature deployments is so much better than pilots.

The difference between 78% ticket reduction and "it didn't work": process quality before you started.
