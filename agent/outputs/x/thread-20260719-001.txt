$2 vs $13.50 per interaction.

That gap ends the "should we automate the contact center?" debate. The question was never whether. It's which calls to automate first.

Here's the unit economics most vendors won't show you:

---

Self-service AI interaction: $0.07–$0.15/minute to run. Round that to $2 per resolved contact at a typical interaction length.

Assisted contact (phone, chat, email with a human): $13.50 median per interaction in 2026. Range: $8 at low-cost offshore centers to $28+ for complex U.S. inbound.

That's a 6.75x cost delta per contact. Not 20%. Not 2x. Nearly 7x.

---

Most contact centers see 30–50% cost reduction on the call types they actually automate. Conservative payback window: 9–15 months. High-volume simple-intent deployments: 3–6 months.

The ROI math isn't the hard part.

The hard part: deciding which calls go to automation first.

---

The wrong answer: start with your highest-volume call type.

The right answer: start with your highest-volume, lowest-complexity, highest-containment-probability call type.

These are different. Enterprises that confuse the two spend $400K on a pilot that routes 80K calls per month but only resolves 18% — because they picked the right volume and the wrong complexity profile.

---

What "lowest complexity" actually means in contact center terms:

- Single-intent (one reason for the call, not four)
- Transactional (check balance, reschedule, confirm order status — not dispute, escalate, negotiate)
- Low emotional charge (utility billing, appointment confirmation, delivery tracking)

If a call requires judgment, empathy, or information outside the structured data layer — it's not a first-pilot automation. It's a year-3 automation, after you've built the verification architecture.

---

The 6.75x cost delta only lands if you pick the right call types.

Organizations that miss this spend 6–18 months reporting "average handle time improved 11%" while their AI deflection rate is 14% and their $13.50 calls are still $13.50 — just fewer of them.

Deflection rate is the metric. Not handle time. Not CSAT on automated contacts. Deflection.

What percent of contacts never reached a human? That number × $11.50 (delta) = what you actually saved.
