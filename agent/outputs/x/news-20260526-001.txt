$300 billion. One quarter. 81% captured by AI.

Q1 2026 just shattered every venture capital record in history. OpenAI alone raised $122B. Anthropic pulled $30B. xAI added $20B. Four companies collectively claimed 65% of all global venture funding in 90 days.

That's not a trend. That's a reallocation event.

Here's what the headline obscures: non-AI startup funding fell 10% while AI funding rose 52%. The capital that used to flow to SaaS, fintech, and healthtech is being redirected — not because those sectors stopped working, but because institutional money has concluded AI is the only asymmetric bet left.

The concentration risk is real and almost nobody's modeling it. When four companies absorb 65% of global venture in one quarter, the remaining 35% has to fund every other technology company on earth. The competition for non-AI venture capital just got structurally harder.

Three things this tells me:

First, the AI infrastructure arms race has no ceiling yet. $300B in Q1 and no signs of slowdown means someone still believes the return justifies the number. Maybe they're right.

Second, the "agentic AI" framing is driving more capital than any other category — specifically companies building systems that do professional work end-to-end, not demos that assist humans. The wrapper era is closing; the deployment era is opening.

Third, enterprise AI budgets are following VC signals. When your CEO reads that 90% of AI investment is going to agentic systems, your IT roadmap discussion changes. CFOs are being asked to allocate agentic AI budgets before most teams know what "agentic" means.

$300B deployed in 90 days is a signal. The question isn't whether AI wins. It's which companies survive the capital concentration long enough to benefit.
