Voice AI responds 24/7 in 45+ languages. AI agents automate 70%+ of support volume in Q1. $80B in labor savings projected by year-end.

And yet 91% of customer service leaders are under executive pressure to implement AI — not already deployed, under pressure TO implement.

That gap is the business story of 2026 contact center technology.

Here's what the gap actually looks like from the inside:

The companies at 70% automation aren't the ones who bought the flashiest AI platform. They're the ones who solved the data problem first. Real-time order data. Live CRM integration. Agent notes accessible mid-conversation. Without that context, the AI can handle greetings and FAQ — it can't handle the conversation.

The companies still in "evaluation mode" are waiting for permission from legal, compliance, IT, and leadership simultaneously. AI that handles 70% of calls has to have access to systems that currently require individual human authorization to touch.

This is the real bottleneck. Not the model quality. Not the cost. Access governance for the data the agent needs to actually resolve the contact.

52% of organizations cite customer service automation as the most transformative use case for voice technology. Those 52% haven't hit the data access problem yet, or they've solved it and are already at 70%.

The other 48% are probably mid-procurement cycle, still negotiating who owns the integration.
