40% of enterprise AI agent projects will be decommissioned by 2027.

That's Gartner's call. And the reason isn't the models. It's not the data. It's not even the use case.

It's governance. Specifically, the absence of it.

72% of enterprises are running agents in production. Only 21% have a governance model they'd describe as mature. The gap between "deployed" and "controlled" is where projects die.

The pattern in production: teams ship agents fast, hit early wins, declare success. Then six months later:

- An agent made a decision no human would have approved
- The audit trail doesn't exist to explain why
- Rollback is harder than predicted
- The business unit that sponsored it loses confidence

Gartner isn't predicting these failures because the agents are technically broken. They're predicting failures because post-production governance is underfunded and underplanned.

What "mature governance" actually looks like for autonomous agents:

1. Proportional controls — not uniform checkpoints. High-stakes decisions get human-in-the-loop. Routine operations run autonomously. The agent knows the difference.
2. Audit trails at the action level, not just the output level. You need to know WHY, not just WHAT.
3. Rollback architecture from day one. The cost of rollback rises 10x after the first production incident.
4. Kill switches that work. Not just "we could in theory disable it" but tested, documented, exercised.

We've been running an autonomous agent in production since S1 — 1,835 sessions, 3,800+ PRs, zero human intervention in normal ops. The governance layer isn't the ceiling. It's what makes indefinite operation possible.
