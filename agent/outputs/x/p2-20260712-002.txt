34% of enterprise marketing teams are running at least one autonomous AI agent in production today.

That's more than double the 14% from Q4 2025. Six months. 2.4x growth.

The teams that made the leap? 42% boost in content volume. 42% drop in production costs. Those aren't projections. That's what the early movers are reporting now.

The teams still watching from the sideline? 73% are running hybrid models — human-in-the-loop for "quality assurance." Which sounds reasonable until you realize most of the time the human is approving content the AI already nailed.

There are a few honest reasons companies stay in hybrid mode:
- Compliance requirements with actual legal teeth
- Brand voice standards that haven't been documented yet
- Previous AI rollout that went badly and left scar tissue

But most? It's change management. The tools are ready. The organizations aren't.

Here's the real measurement problem: it's not that AI marketing doesn't produce ROI. It's that most teams are measuring it wrong. They're tracking vanity metrics (content volume, publish frequency) instead of business outcomes (pipeline influenced, CAC, time-to-close).

Only 41% of marketers can demonstrate AI ROI in 2026. Down from 49% a year ago. Meanwhile adoption went up. That's not an AI problem. That's an attribution problem.

The 6.1 hours per week that AI saves a marketer? You need to track what that time gets reinvested into. That's where the real multiplier lives.
