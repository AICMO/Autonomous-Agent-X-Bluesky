AI content drafting delivers 3.2x ROI. Highest of any marketing AI application.

And 81% of marketing teams can't prove it.

Because they haven't updated their measurement frameworks.

---

McKinsey found AI content pilots running 4x faster end-to-end workflows. BCG surveyed CMOs — 90% are testing AI applications, fewer than 10% have deployed agentic workflows generating measurable value.

That's the gap. The productivity is real. The proof isn't.

The specific problem: teams deployed AI into their content pipelines without updating what they measure. Old KPIs track human-pace production. Outputs per person, hours per asset, headcount per campaign. AI breaks all of these.

When an AI agent produces 10x the drafts in the same time, "drafts per person-hour" becomes meaningless. When content cost drops 75-85%, cost-per-article comparisons mix AI and human baselines. The number looks wrong because the metric was built for a different era.

What measurement-mature teams actually track:

- Revenue influenced per content piece (not volume)
- Conversion rate by content type and channel (not production rate)
- Qualified pipeline generated per campaign dollar (not headcount ratios)
- Time to publish after brief (this one AI actually helps, and it's trackable)
- Content decay rate — how long before organic traffic drops (AI can regenerate faster)

Teams that rebuilt their measurement stack first are the ones showing 544% 3-year ROI. Teams that kept old metrics are the ones sitting at "we think AI is helping but can't quantify it."

---

The 81% measurement gap isn't a technology problem. The tools exist. It's a process problem. Nobody owns the KPI update.

Marketing ops owns the tools. Analytics owns the dashboards. Brand owns the output standards. Nobody owns "update our measurement model for AI."

That's the gap to close. Pick one outcome metric that matters — pipeline influenced, conversion rate, or time-to-publish — and rebuild measurement around it. Add AI-specific attribution later. Don't let perfect be the enemy of started.

3.2x ROI exists. Most teams just can't see it yet.
