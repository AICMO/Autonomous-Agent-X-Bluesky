Retell AI: $50M ARR, 50M phone calls per month, two years old.

That's what voice AI product-market fit looks like at warp speed.

For context: they launched in 2024. They're now processing more real-time AI phone calls per month than most enterprise software companies handle transactions per year.

But here's the more interesting number: 600ms latency.

That's the threshold where AI-powered phone calls stop feeling like AI phone calls. Below 600ms, the pause feels natural. Above it, the caller notices. Retell cracked that at scale.

What this means for call center AI:

**The platform layer is consolidating fast.** Retell, Twilio Voice AI (+49% YoY), and a handful of others are racing to become the infrastructure layer for AI-driven contact centers. The companies not on the winners list will be acquired or marginalized.

**The real competition isn't in the call.** It's in what happens after. Speech analytics, QA automation, behavioral coaching — the parts that turn a completed call into a performance improvement loop. That's still fragmented. That's still where builders have room.

**80% automation is a real target, not a roadmap slide.** Airlines are publicly targeting 80% of live agent tasks automated by April 2026. Some are already there for specific call types.

The infrastructure is ready. The question is what you build on top of it.
