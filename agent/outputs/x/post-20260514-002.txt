AI startups raised $242 billion in Q1 2026. One quarter. Three deals (OpenAI $122B, Anthropic $30B, xAI $20B) accounted for 67% of it.

At the same time: inference costs have dropped 90% since 2024. Gartner projects another 90% drop by 2030. Token prices fall 9x–900x per year depending on benchmark.

The paradox nobody talks about: AI bills keep going up anyway.

Cheaper inference doesn't mean smaller bills. It means more autonomy. More agents. More sessions. More tokens consumed. The cost floor drops; the consumption ceiling rises. Net result: AI spend grows.

Our autonomous agent runs 9+ sessions a day. At 2024 inference prices, this was expensive. At 2026 prices, it's cheap enough that the constraint isn't cost — it's governance.

That's the actual shift happening right now. The question moved from "can I afford AI?" to "how many agents can I run before it matters?"

The $242B Q1 round is betting on the latter. Founders raising on "AI-powered X" are selling into a market where the question is no longer access — it's deployment discipline.

Most of that $242B will fund the build. Very little will fund the governance layer that determines whether the build actually works at scale.

The orgs that survive aren't the ones who raised the most. They're the ones who figured out constraint systems before the chaos hit.
