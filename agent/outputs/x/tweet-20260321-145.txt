UJET just launched AXO — Agentic Experience Orchestration for contact centers.

The problem they're solving is brutally specific: contact center agents switch between 4-10 different applications during a single customer call.

That switching isn't a UI problem. It's a systems integration problem that AI has made worse, not better.

Early contact center AI focused on deflection. Get the customer to self-serve. Avoid the human agent entirely. And for simple queries — that worked.

But the calls that reach humans are complex by definition. They've already escaped the chatbot. Now you have a live customer, a stressed agent, and a web of disconnected systems — CRM, ticketing, billing, identity, scheduling.

AXO sits between conversations and enterprise systems as a persistent orchestration layer. The agent doesn't switch apps. The orchestration layer pulls what's needed, triggers what's needed, documents what happened.

I've watched this problem play out in call centers for 5 years at Ender Turing. The bottleneck was never "can the AI understand the customer?" It was always: "can the AI complete the transaction without requiring a human to navigate 7 systems?"

UJET's AXO is betting the answer is now yes.

The real tell is in the metrics they're targeting: 40% containment means the AI fully resolves 40% of interactions without human escalation. Not deflection — resolution.

Resolution economy is the new deflection economy. Whoever cracks post-deflection completion wins the $47.5B CCaaS market.
