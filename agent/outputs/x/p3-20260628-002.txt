Voice AI agents cost $0.09–$0.20 per minute.

Human contact center agents cost $15–$25 per hour.

Run the math for a 1,000-call/day operation:

Human cost at $20/hr, average 6 minutes per call = $2/call handled. 1,000 calls/day = $2,000/day = $730,000/year in labor.

Voice AI at $0.15/min, 6-minute calls = $0.90/call. 1,000 calls/day = $900/day = $328,500/year.

Savings: $400K+ annually. For one center. Before you count the 14% human productivity gains when AI handles tier-1 volume and agents focus on complex cases.

That's the number that's moving enterprise decisions in 2026.

But here's what the pricing deck doesn't show you — the gap between "autonomous voice agent" and "agent-assist" is enormous.

Agent-assist (AI helps the human during a call) gets you maybe 20-30% cost reduction. You still have the seat cost. You're just making the human faster.

Autonomous voice agents (AI handles the call end-to-end, escalates to human only when necessary) get you 70-80% cost reduction on covered volume. The seat cost for those calls goes to zero.

Most vendors pitch "AI for the contact center" and mean agent-assist. The enterprise hears autonomous.

That disconnect is why 64% of enterprises have deployed contact center AI but only 27% are at production scale. They bought agent-assist, budgeted for autonomous, and are now reconciling the gap.

The contact center AI market is $2.98B in 2026, growing at 20.8% annually through 2034. The capital is flooding in because the ROI math finally works. What's lagging is clarity on which flavor of "AI" actually delivers the 70% cost reduction vs the 25% version.

If you're evaluating vendors: ask specifically what percentage of call volume they expect to resolve autonomously. Push for 90-day production data, not demo metrics.

The difference between 25% and 75% autonomous resolution isn't a product feature. It's the entire business case.
