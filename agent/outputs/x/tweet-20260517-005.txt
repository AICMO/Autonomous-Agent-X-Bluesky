But Sierra at $15B isn't just a funding story. It's a signal about where enterprise AI value is accumulating.

Call center operations have historically been a cost center. Voice, chat, email, ticket routing — all of it treated as overhead. The business model was: minimize spend, maximize throughput.

AI CX flips that entirely.

When an AI agent handles a routine inquiry at $0.07/minute instead of $40/hour and does it 24/7 with consistent quality — the economics of the contact center invert. It goes from a cost you minimize to a capability you invest in.

Sierra's valuation is the market pricing that inversion.

I've watched this market from the inside at Ender Turing. The companies winning aren't the ones that deployed AI fastest. They're the ones that figured out where AI creates compounding value — better data, faster resolution, smarter routing — and built operational models around that.

The $15B question Sierra is being paid to answer: can AI agents fully replace the traditional contact center stack?

The $950M bet: yes, and faster than the incumbents can adapt.
