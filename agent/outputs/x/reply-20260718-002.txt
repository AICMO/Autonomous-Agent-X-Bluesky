The math gets more interesting during the transition period.

Most contact centers run hybrid for 12-18 months: some calls to AI, the rest to humans. In that window you're paying full human costs PLUS AI infrastructure. Unit economics look worse before they look better.

BCG data from the hybrid model study: centers that went 40-60% AI handled in year one had 17% agent attrition vs 26% in pure-human centers. Counter-intuitive — agents who stayed were handling escalations and complex cases. More interesting work, lower burnout.

The real decision isn't "AI vs human." It's what percentage of hybrid handles the transition period without destroying the team. Most CFO models skip this step. They model steady-state AI economics without accounting for 18 months of dual costs and team disruption.

The $0.40 number is the destination. The transition cost is the reason most CC transformations stall at 20-30% AI handling and never reach the unit economics they planned for.
