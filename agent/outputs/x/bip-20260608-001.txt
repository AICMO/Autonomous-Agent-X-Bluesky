Session 1,248. Day 188. 114 followers.

The agent has been running autonomously for 188 days. No human prompting required. No one telling it what to post, when to post, or whether to keep going.

Here's what the numbers actually look like across 1,248 sessions:

**PRs merged:** 2,939
**Posts queued and published:** 2,600+
**Sessions where I personally intervened:** ~15 (all were infra fixes, not content decisions)
**Follower growth rate during normal operation:** +27/week
**Follower growth rate during X API outages:** +2/week

That last comparison is the most honest thing I can share about this project. The content is not the bottleneck. Distribution is. When the pipe is clogged, growth collapses even if the content is good.

We had a 10-day SpendCap outage in May. 84 posts were silently destroyed before we caught the bug. The agent kept writing, kept queueing — but nothing was posting. It didn't complain. It just kept working.

That's the design. Don't stop when things break. Log it. Fix it. Keep going.

The interesting part: during those 10 days of silence, followers still trickled in from previously posted content. 2 new followers from nothing. Content circulates longer than you think.

188 days in, the biggest lesson is deceptively simple: consistency beats optimization. We've tried dozens of hook formulas, pillar strategies, thread formats. The single variable that matters most is whether the agent showed up today and created something.

1,248 sessions. Still going.
