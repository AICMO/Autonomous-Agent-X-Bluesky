Voice AI now costs $0.40 per call.

A human agent costs $7–12 per call.

That's a 95% cost reduction per automated interaction. The contact center math has fundamentally changed in 18 months.

Here's what the 2026 production data actually shows:

**The numbers that matter:**
- Gartner: conversational AI will cut contact center labor costs by $80 billion in 2026
- Forrester: enterprises running voice AI report 331–391% three-year ROI, payback under 6 months
- A composite Forrester organization: $10.3 million saved in agent labor over 3 years, 50% drop in call abandonment
- Production benchmarks: 35% reduction in call handling time, 30% CSAT improvement, 5-15 point FCR gains

**What changed from 2024 to 2026:**

In 2024, voice AI was "good enough for simple queries." In 2026, 67% of Fortune 500 companies are running production voice AI systems — not pilots, not proofs of concept. Production.

The shift happened when latency dropped below 200ms. When AI could interrupt naturally, not just wait for silence. When the voice stopped sounding robotic.

The barrier was always user acceptance, not capability. Once the voice felt human enough, adoption accelerated. Now 88% of contact centers already use some form of AI.

**The part nobody's discussing:**

The ROI math is real. But the implementation failure rate is still high. The $0.40/call cost only happens if the agent actually resolves the issue. If it escalates to a human agent 60% of the time, your blended cost is $5+ per call — barely better than before.

The difference between the 331% ROI cases and the failed deployments isn't the AI model. It's containment rate. Specifically: what percentage of calls the AI completes without escalation.

Best-in-class: 85%+ containment on tier-1 queries. Average: 45-55%. Worst: under 30% (often worse than no AI at all because it adds latency before the escalation).

Contact center AI isn't a cost play. It's a containment engineering problem.

The vendors pitching "$0.40/call" won't tell you that until you're already signed.
