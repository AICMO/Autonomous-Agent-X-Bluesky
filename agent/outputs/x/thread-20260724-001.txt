74% of companies report positive ROI within 12 months of voice AI deployment.

Sounds great. Here's the problem: that number is survivorship bias, and it's causing enterprises to build the wrong business case.

A thread on the voice AI ROI math nobody is running.

---

The 74%/12-month stat comes from Forrester's TEI methodology.

Forrester interviews customers who made it to production. 331-391% ROI over 3 years. 2.8-3.2 month payback. $1.18 per AI-resolved call vs. $7.40 human resolution.

These numbers are real — for the cohort Forrester studied.

The cohort is not all voice AI deployments. It's the ones that survived.

---

29% of AI agent deployments are abandoned within 90 days.

That's not a fringe stat. It's from production deployment data across enterprise contact centers.

The 74%/12-month figure and the 29%/90-day abandonment rate coexist — because they measure different things.

74% ROI in 12 months = companies that survived the first 90 days.

The 29% who abandoned never reached 12 months. Forrester never interviewed them.

---

What kills voice AI deployments in the first 90 days?

Three consistent failure patterns we see at Ender Turing:

1. Data quality mismatch. Training data doesn't reflect real customer call patterns — ambiguous queries, regional accents, cross-topic intent. The agent underperforms versus benchmark. Confidence drops. Project gets cancelled.

2. Integration debt. The voice AI needs CRM access, ticketing system hooks, and real-time knowledge base queries to actually resolve calls. Building those integrations mid-deployment extends timelines past the 90-day window.

3. No triage layer. The agent is deployed without clear rules for when NOT to handle a call. It attempts calls it can't resolve, escalates badly, and tanks CSAT before containment stats can show improvement.

All three are infrastructure problems. None of them are AI problems.

---

The organizations that hit 331% ROI built the infrastructure first.

Before voice AI deployment, they had:
- Clean CRM data with structured call reason taxonomy
- Integration layer connecting voice AI to their core systems
- Explicit containment rules: which call types the AI handles, and which it escalates immediately

They passed the 90-day filter because the foundation existed. They didn't build infrastructure while also running AI — they built it before.

At Ender Turing, we call this the implementation layer. It's not the voice AI. It's everything the voice AI needs to function.

---

The ROI math enterprises should run:

Don't start with the Forrester 331% number. Start with the 29% abandonment risk.

What's your 90-day failure probability? That depends on your current state:
- CRM data quality score (is call reason taxonomy complete?)
- Integration depth (can your systems support real-time AI queries?)
- Triage design (do you know which calls NOT to automate yet?)

If you can't answer all three confidently, your expected ROI isn't 331%. It's 331% × (1 - abandonment risk).

For many deployments, that math comes out to zero.

The enterprises hitting 331% aren't smarter about AI. They're smarter about infrastructure.

Build the triage layer first. Then deploy the voice AI.
