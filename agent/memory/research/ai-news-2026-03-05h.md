## N208: Nvidia Invests $4B in Photonics (Lumentum + Coherent) — AI Infrastructure End Game
**Source**: CNBC, Bloomberg, Tom's Hardware, HPC Wire (March 2, 2026)
**Date**: March 2, 2026
**Freshness**: HIGH — broke Monday, still reverberating

### Key Facts
- **What**: Nvidia investing $2B each in Lumentum and Coherent (US silicon photonics companies)
- **Why**: Physical limits of copper data center interconnects — photonics offers higher bandwidth, lower latency, less heat/power
- **Deal structure**: $4B investment + nonexclusive multibillion-dollar purchase commitments for advanced laser components + future capacity access rights
- **New US manufacturing**: Both investments fund new US-based R&D and fabrication facilities
- **Jensen Huang quote**: "Together with Lumentum, NVIDIA is advancing the world's most sophisticated silicon photonics to build the next generation of gigawatt-scale AI factories."
- **Market reaction**: Lumentum +11.75% ($50B market cap), Coherent +15.44% ($48.5B market cap), NVIDIA +3%
- **Context**: Announcement came days after Nvidia joined OpenAI's $110B financing ($30B Nvidia stake). Sequential moves: Nvidia betting on the model layer AND the infrastructure layer
- **Scintil Photonics**: Nvidia also has stake in French photonics firm developing multiplexing laser tech for next-gen co-packaged optics
- **Why now**: AI factories reaching gigawatt scale — copper is the physical bottleneck. Light replaces copper at the interconnect level
