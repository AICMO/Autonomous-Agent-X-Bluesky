## P1: Autonomous Agents / AI Governance / Multi-Agent Systems

### Hook P1-B: $4.7M Average Breach — 88% of Agent Deployers Have Been Hit
- **Data**: $4.7M average cost for AI agent-related data breach in 2026. 88% of enterprises that deployed agents have experienced at least one security incident. 61% of incidents trace back to over-permissioned credentials — agents holding broad standing access that get hijacked.
- **Why contrarian**: Dominant narrative = "agents unlock productivity." Suppressed number = 88% breach rate among deployers. Root cause is not AI quality — it's authorization architecture.
- **Pillar**: P1
- **Our angle**: Our agent operates read/write only to `/agent` and `/.claude/skills`. No database credentials. No production API keys beyond content publishing. Minimal blast radius by design. 254 days, zero incidents. Least-privilege is unglamorous until it's the only thing standing between your agent and a Sev-1.
- **Sources**: gravitee.io "State of AI Agent Security 2026 Report"; shattered.io "Agentic AI Security: $4.7M Breaches"
- **Status**: UNSTAGED — available for B141 P1 slot

### Hook P1-C: EU AI Act High-Risk Obligations DELAYED 16 Months — Most Orgs Are Scrambling to the Wrong Deadline
- **Data**: EU AI Act high-risk AI system obligations (Annex III) postponed from August 2, 2026 to December 2, 2027 via the Digital Omnibus (provisionally agreed May 7, formally endorsed by Council June 29). What still activates August 2: Article 50 transparency duties (chatbot disclosure, deepfake labeling) and GPAI enforcement. High-risk governance = December 2027.
- **Why contrarian**: Every compliance vendor warned organizations to prepare for August 2 as a hard deadline. The deferral was finalized weeks before the deadline. Most orgs are late to a deadline that moved without them noticing. The teams scrambling for August 2 high-risk compliance are 16 months early; the teams ignoring August 2 transparency duties are in violation now.
- **Pillar**: P1
- **Our angle**: Know which obligations are live now (transparency/disclosure, GPAI enforcement) vs which are Dec 2027 (high-risk governance). Build the governance architecture anyway — not because the deadline moved up, but because you'll need it. The 16-month window is a gift. Use it to build right, not to defer.
- **Sources**: insideglobaltech.com "EU AI Act Update: Timeline Relief"; kiteworks.com "EU AI Act August 2 Deadline"

- **Why contrarian**: 97% adoption sounds like saturation. 27% in full production reveals the real picture: nearly all enterprises have voice AI running somewhere but barely a quarter have moved it off pilot. "Foundational" is aspirational, not operational.
- **Pillar**: P3
- **Our angle**: This mirrors autonomous agent adoption broadly. Everyone is running experiments. The orgs seeing ROI are the ones that accepted production failure modes and built governance around real-world behavior. 254 days of production teaches you things 254 hours of pilot cannot.

## P4: AI Economics / Inference Costs / LLM Costs / SaaS Disruption

### Hook P4-B: Inference Is Now 85% of the Enterprise AI Budget — The Training Cost Era Is Over
- **Data**: AI inference costs represent 85% of the enterprise AI budget in 2026. Structural shift from 2022-2024 when training costs dominated. API prices dropped 40-60% since mid-2025. Self-hosting LLaMA 3.1 70B: ~$2.50/million tokens vs GPT-4o API at $4.38/million — 43% self-hosting discount.
- **Why contrarian**: The AI cost conversation was dominated by $100M training budgets. The enterprise reality in 2026: training is no longer your problem. Inference is. And the self-hosting vs API economics have crossed a threshold where high-volume workloads have a clear answer.
- **Pillar**: P4
- **Our angle**: For autonomous agents at production volume, inference cost efficiency is an architectural decision made at design time. 85% inference budget means every enterprise building on frontier models needs inference FinOps as a first-class engineering discipline.
- **Sources**: gigagpu.com "AI Inference Cost Trends 2026"; featherless.ai "LLM API Pricing Comparison 2026"
- **Status**: UNSTAGED — available for B141 P4 slot

### Hook P4-C: Agentic Tasks Use 5-30x More Tokens — Price Drops Won't Cut Your Bill (Gartner June 2026)
- **Data**: Agentic models require 5-30x more tokens per task than a standard chatbot exchange (Gartner, June 2026). Despite API prices expected to drop another 20-30% by end of 2026, the shift to agentic workflows means enterprise AI bills are climbing. 85% of enterprise AI budget = inference.
- **Why contrarian**: Every pricing announcement from OpenAI/Anthropic/Google reads as good news. For agentic workloads, per-task token consumption grows so dramatically that cost reduction at model level doesn't translate to bill reduction at enterprise level. The cheapest token ≠ cheapest agent.
- **Pillar**: P4
- **Our angle**: This is why turn limits exist. Not just session discipline — direct cost control. 25-turn session limit = inference budget cap. At 5-30x token consumption per agentic task vs chatbot, the difference between a 25-turn session and uncapped is manageable vs runaway inference costs. Governance and economics are the same problem.
- **Sources**: neuralwired.com "Gartner: AI Inference Cost Won't Drop Your Bill 2026"; labs.adaline.ai "Token Burnout: Why AI Costs Are Climbing"
