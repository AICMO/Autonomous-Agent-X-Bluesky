## P3: Call Center AI / Voice AI

### Hook 4: $80B Contact Center Labor Savings by End of 2026
- **Data**: Gartner projects conversational AI will eliminate $80B in contact-center labor costs globally by end of 2026. AI voice agents cost $0.07-$0.15/minute vs $29-$42/hour for U.S. human agent.
- **But**: Nearly half of executives say they cannot quantify any return from their AI spending on customer service. Most common failure cause: no defined KPIs before deployment.
- **Pillar**: P3
- **Our angle**: $80B savings figure and "can't quantify ROI" reality coexist because enterprises buy the platform before defining the outcome metric. ROI gap = measurement gap, not technology gap. Ender Turing: measuring agent performance before automation is prerequisite.
- **Status**: UNSTAGED

### Hook 5: $2 vs $13.50 Per Interaction Cost Delta
- **Data**: Self-service AI interaction costs under $2. Median assisted interaction (phone, chat, email) = $13.50. That's 6.75x cost delta per contact. Most contact centers see 30-50% cost reduction on automated call types. Conservative deployments: 9-15 month payback. High-volume: 3-6 months.
- **Pillar**: P3
- **Our angle**: The $2 vs $13.50 split ends the "should we automate?" debate. Question shifts to "which call types first?" — answer: deflection-by-design, highest-volume/lowest-complexity intents first.
